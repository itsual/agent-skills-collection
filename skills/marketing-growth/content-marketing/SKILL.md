# Content Marketing

## Overview

Content marketing earns attention by being useful. It supports awareness, consideration, and retention when tied to real audience problems and a clear distribution plan.

## When to Use

- Building or refreshing a content engine
- Supporting SEO and organic demand generation
- Enabling sales with educational assets
- Establishing thought leadership in a category

## Core Practices

- Start from audience jobs, questions, and search intent
- Map content to funnel stages and product narratives
- Prioritize quality and distribution over volume alone
- Repurpose core ideas across formats (long-form, social, email, video)
- Measure leading indicators (engagement, subscribers) and lagging ones (pipeline, revenue)

## Principles

- Helpfulness is the growth strategy
- Consistency compounds; sporadic brilliance does not
- Distribution is part of the work, not an afterthought
- Refresh and prune; outdated content hurts trust

## Verification

- [ ] Content topics map to real audience needs
- [ ] Each major piece has a distribution plan
- [ ] Success metrics are defined beyond vanity pageviews

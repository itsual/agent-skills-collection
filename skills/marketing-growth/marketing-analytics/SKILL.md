# Marketing Analytics

## Overview

Marketing analytics connects activity to outcomes. Good measurement supports decisions about where to invest, what to cut, and what to learn next.

## When to Use

- Channel and campaign performance review
- Budget allocation decisions
- Funnel diagnosis
- Building marketing dashboards and reporting cadences

## Core Practices

- Define north-star and channel-specific metrics
- Separate leading indicators from lagging revenue outcomes
- Understand attribution limitations; use multiple lenses
- Track cohort and quality of acquired users, not only volume
- Build repeatable reporting with clear owners

## Principles

- Optimize for business outcomes, not platform vanity metrics
- Perfect attribution is rare — decide with directional truth
- Measure quality (retention, LTV) of acquired traffic
- Dashboards should drive decisions, not just decorate meetings

## Verification

- [ ] Metrics map to marketing goals
- [ ] Data sources and definitions are trusted
- [ ] Reports change budget or creative decisions

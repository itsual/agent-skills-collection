# Growth Experimentation

## Overview

Growth experimentation applies the scientific method to growth levers. The goal is not endless testing — it’s faster learning about what moves meaningful metrics.

## When to Use

- Building or maturing a growth team practice
- Exploring new acquisition or activation levers
- Improving retention or monetization mechanically
- Reducing opinion-driven roadmap debates

## Core Practices

- Maintain a prioritized experiment backlog
- Write hypotheses with metric, audience, and expected direction
- Design minimum viable tests
- Ensure measurement integrity before launch
- Share results openly, including failures
- Compound learnings into playbooks

## Principles

- Learning velocity compounds into advantage
- Test the riskiest assumptions first
- Don’t A/B test what you can decide with logic or research
- Protect the user experience while experimenting

## Verification

- [ ] Experiments have explicit hypotheses and success criteria
- [ ] Results are documented and shared
- [ ] Wins are productized; losses inform future bets

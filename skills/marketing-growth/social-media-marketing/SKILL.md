# Social Media Marketing

## Overview

Social media is a conversation channel, distribution surface, and brand stage. Effective social work ties platform behavior to clear business or community goals.

## When to Use

- Building or refining social strategy
- Launch and campaign amplification
- Community building and support
- Thought leadership and employer brand

## Core Practices

- Choose platforms based on where the audience actually is
- Define content pillars aligned with positioning
- Design for native formats (short video, threads, carousels, etc.)
- Engage — don’t only broadcast
- Track engagement quality, referral traffic, and pipeline influence where possible

## Principles

- Platform-native beats cross-posted sludge
- Consistency builds familiarity; quality keeps attention
- Community trust is fragile — respond like a human
- Not every brand needs to be everywhere

## Verification

- [ ] Platforms and goals are explicitly matched
- [ ] Content pillars are clear and repeatable
- [ ] Metrics go beyond follower count

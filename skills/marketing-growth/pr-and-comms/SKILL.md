# PR and Comms

## Overview

PR builds credibility through third-party validation. It works best when the story is real, the timing is right, and relationships are maintained before you need them.

## When to Use

- Product or funding announcements
- Thought leadership and bylines
- Crisis or reputation management
- Analyst relations and industry positioning

## Core Practices

- Define the narrative and newsworthiness honestly
- Build media lists and relationships continuously
- Prepare press kits, FAQs, and spokesperson briefings
- Coordinate embargoes and exclusive offers carefully
- Measure share of voice, quality of coverage, and referral impact
- Plan for reactive comms, not only proactive launches

## Principles

- No amount of PR fixes a weak story
- Credibility > volume of mentions
- Speed and accuracy both matter in crises
- Align PR narrative with product and brand strategy

## Verification

- [ ] Story has a clear “why now” and proof
- [ ] Spokespeople are prepared
- [ ] Success criteria go beyond “number of hits”

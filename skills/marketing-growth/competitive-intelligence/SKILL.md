# Competitive Intelligence

## Overview

Competitive intelligence turns public and field signals into decisions. It should sharpen your differentiation and sales conversations, not distract from customers.

## When to Use

- Building or updating battle cards
- Responding to competitor launches or pricing moves
- Positioning and roadmap input
- Sales enablement against specific alternatives

## Core Practices

- Track product, pricing, messaging, and GTM motions
- Source from public sites, reviews, win/loss, and sales notes
- Synthesize into implications — not just screenshots
- Keep battle cards short and situational
- Refresh on a cadence; archive stale intel

## Principles

- Customers > competitors as the primary focus
- Win/loss truth beats marketing-site claims
- Distinguish table stakes from real differentiators
- Share intel in forms sales will actually use

## Verification

- [ ] Insights change messaging or sales behavior
- [ ] Battle cards are current and concise
- [ ] Sources are credible and dated

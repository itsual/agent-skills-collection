# Community Marketing

## Overview

Community turns customers into participants. Done well, it produces support deflection, product insight, retention, and authentic advocacy.

## When to Use

- Building user groups, forums, Discord/Slack communities
- Community-led growth or education programs
- Champion and ambassador initiatives
- Category-building around shared practices

## Core Practices

- Define the community’s purpose beyond “marketing channel”
- Seed valuable content and rituals early
- Empower moderators and member leaders
- Create recognition paths for contributors
- Listen systematically and close the loop to product
- Measure health: activity, retention, sentiment — not vanity size alone

## Principles

- Community is a relationship, not a broadcast list
- Give more value than you extract
- Moderation and safety are product features
- Don’t force community where the product doesn’t support ongoing shared practice

## Verification

- [ ] Members have a clear reason to return
- [ ] Feedback reaches the product team
- [ ] Health metrics are tracked beyond member count

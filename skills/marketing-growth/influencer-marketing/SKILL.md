# Influencer Marketing

## Overview

Influencer marketing borrows trust. It works when the creator’s audience aligns with your buyer and the content fits the creator’s voice — not when the message feels forced.

## When to Use

- Product launches targeting niche communities
- Ongoing creator affiliate or ambassador programs
- Category education through trusted voices
- Social proof campaigns

## Core Practices

- Prioritize audience fit and engagement quality over follower count
- Brief for outcomes and guardrails, not rigid scripts
- Disclose partnerships clearly
- Provide product access and creative freedom within brand safety limits
- Track codes, UTMs, and assisted conversions
- Build longer-term relationships with high performers

## Principles

- Authenticity is the channel’s advantage — don’t kill it
- Micro-influencers often convert better in B2B and niche B2C
- One-off posts rarely beat sustained partnerships
- Brand safety and disclosure are non-negotiable

## Verification

- [ ] Creator audience matches ICP
- [ ] Disclosure is clear
- [ ] Measurement captures more than vanity views

# Lifecycle Marketing

## Overview

Lifecycle marketing meets people where they are in their relationship with the product — with the right message, channel, and timing for each stage.

## When to Use

- Onboarding and activation programs
- Retention and engagement campaigns
- Expansion and upsell journeys
- Win-back and re-activation flows

## Core Practices

- Map stages and defining events (signup, activation, habit, churn risk)
- Trigger messages from behavior, not only calendars
- Coordinate email, in-product, push, and sales touches
- Personalize by segment and behavior depth
- Suppress noise; respect frequency caps
- Measure stage conversion and incremental impact

## Principles

- Behavior beats batch-and-blast
- Each stage has a different job for marketing
- Lifecycle and product experience must reinforce each other
- Opt-downs and preferences preserve long-term reach

## Verification

- [ ] Stages and triggers are explicitly defined
- [ ] Journeys have clear success metrics per stage
- [ ] Channels are orchestrated, not conflicting

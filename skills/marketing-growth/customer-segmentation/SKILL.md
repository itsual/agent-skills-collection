# Customer Segmentation

## Overview

Segmentation turns a market into actionable groups. Useful segments differ in needs, behaviors, or value — and therefore in how you message, price, or serve them.

## When to Use

- Targeting campaigns and content
- Lifecycle and retention marketing
- Sales territory or motion design
- Personalization strategy

## Core Practices

- Choose segmentation bases that change decisions (needs, behavior, value, firmographics)
- Validate segments with data and qualitative insight
- Keep the set small enough to operationalize
- Assign clear messaging and offers per priority segment
- Revisit segments as the market and product evolve

## Principles

- Segments must be measurable, accessible, and actionable
- Avoid segments that are interesting but unusable operationally
- Behavior and needs often beat pure demographics
- Not every segment deserves equal investment

## Verification

- [ ] Segments drive different actions (message, channel, offer)
- [ ] Teams can identify which segment a customer belongs to
- [ ] Priority segments are resourced intentionally

# Retention Marketing

## Overview

Retention marketing keeps customers successful and aware of value they might underuse. It complements product retention work with education, engagement, and timely saves.

## When to Use

- Rising churn or contraction
- Low feature adoption among existing customers
- Expansion and cross-sell programs
- Renewal and win-back campaigns

## Core Practices

- Segment by health, usage, and lifecycle risk
- Educate toward underused value tied to outcomes
- Trigger interventions on risk signals
- Coordinate CS, product, and marketing touches
- Run renewal and expansion plays with clear owners
- Measure retention, NRR, and campaign incremental impact

## Principles

- The best retention marketing is product value realized
- Don’t spam inactive users with generic “we miss you”
- Expansion works best when tied to proven success
- Save offers should address the real reason for leaving

## Verification

- [ ] Risk segments and triggers are defined
- [ ] Programs tie to product value, not only discounts
- [ ] Impact on retention/NRR is measured

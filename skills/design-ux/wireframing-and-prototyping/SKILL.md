# Wireframing and Prototyping

## Overview

Wireframes and prototypes are thinking tools. The goal is learning and alignment, not pixel perfection at every stage.

## When to Use

- Exploring layout and flow options quickly
- Aligning product, design, and engineering on structure
- Preparing interactive prototypes for usability tests
- Reducing expensive rework later in the cycle

## Fidelity Guidance

- **Low** — Structure, hierarchy, flow (fast, disposable)
- **Mid** — Key interactions and content priority
- **High** — Visual design + interaction detail for final validation or handoff

## Principles

- Match fidelity to the question you’re answering
- Prototype the riskiest or least-understood interactions first
- Keep early artifacts easy to change
- Annotate intent when static wireframes hide behavior
- Don’t polish work that hasn’t been validated

## Verification

- [ ] Artifact answers a specific design or product question
- [ ] Stakeholders understand what is decided vs still open
- [ ] Prototype is sufficient for the planned test or discussion

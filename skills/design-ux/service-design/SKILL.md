# Service Design

## Overview

Service design looks beyond a single interface to the full system of people, processes, and touchpoints that deliver value. It connects customer experience with operational reality.

## When to Use

- Multi-channel or omnichannel experiences
- Services involving human staff + digital product
- Complex onboarding, support, or fulfillment journeys
- Aligning product, operations, and support around one experience

## Core Practices

- Map the full service journey (frontstage and backstage)
- Identify pain points, handoffs, and ownership gaps
- Design service blueprints
- Prototype service experiences, not only UI
- Align metrics across channels and teams

## Principles

- The experience is only as strong as the weakest handoff
- Backstage processes shape frontstage quality
- Design for employees as well as customers when staff deliver the service
- Orchestrate, don’t only optimize isolated touchpoints

## Verification

- [ ] Journey includes cross-channel and human touchpoints
- [ ] Backstage ownership is clear for critical steps
- [ ] Improvements address systemic issues, not only surface UI

# Design Thinking

## Overview

Design thinking is a structured approach to creative problem-solving that keeps users at the center and treats ideas as hypotheses to be tested.

## When to Use

- Ambiguous or poorly framed problems
- Cross-functional ideation and alignment workshops
- Early exploration before committing to a solution path
- Building empathy and shared understanding on a team

## Core Phases

1. **Empathize** — Understand users and context
2. **Define** — Frame the right problem
3. **Ideate** — Generate a range of possible solutions
4. **Prototype** — Make ideas tangible quickly
5. **Test** — Learn from real reactions and iterate

## Principles

- Bias toward action and learning over endless discussion
- Divergent then convergent thinking — don’t mix them carelessly
- Reframe problems; the initial brief is often incomplete
- Prototypes exist to learn, not to impress

## Verification

- [ ] Problem framing is grounded in user insight
- [ ] Multiple options were explored before converging
- [ ] Ideas were tested in some tangible form

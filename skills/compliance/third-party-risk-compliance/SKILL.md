# Third-Party Risk Compliance

## Overview

Regulators and customers still hold you accountable when vendors fail. Third-party compliance risk management extends controls beyond the enterprise boundary.

## When to Use

- Vendor onboarding for material or regulated services
- Outsourcing and cloud arrangements
- Periodic vendor risk reviews
- Contractual compliance clause design

## Core Practices

- Tier vendors by inherent risk (data, criticality, regulation)
- Perform proportionate due diligence before reliance
- Embed audit rights, security, privacy, and subcontractor controls in contracts
- Monitor performance, incidents, and control attestations
- Plan exit and substitutability for critical vendors
- Maintain an inventory of material third parties

## Principles

- You can outsource work, not accountability
- One-time questionnaires are not ongoing oversight
- Concentration risk (one vendor, one region) is a compliance issue too
- Fourth parties (subprocessors) matter for data and resilience

## Verification

- [ ] Material vendors are inventoried and tiered
- [ ] Diligence and contracts match risk tier
- [ ] Ongoing oversight cadence exists for critical vendors

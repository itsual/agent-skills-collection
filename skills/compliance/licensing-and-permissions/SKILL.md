# Licensing and Permissions

## Overview

Many activities require explicit regulatory authorization. Operating outside the permissions perimeter creates severe enforcement risk.

## When to Use

- New product or business line launches
- Geographic expansion
- Changes to regulated activity scope
- Permissions inventory and condition tracking

## Core Practices

- Maintain an inventory of licenses, permissions, and conditions
- Map business activities to authorized permissions
- Assess whether new initiatives require variation or new licenses
- Track regulatory conditions and reporting attached to permissions
- Engage legal/compliance before crossing the perimeter
- Prepare application quality materials when seeking authorization

## Principles

- “Everyone else does it” is not authorization
- Perimeter questions should be asked early in product design
- Conditions are binding — not advisory footnotes
- Change in business model can change licensing status

## Verification

- [ ] Permissions inventory is complete and current
- [ ] Material initiatives get perimeter assessment
- [ ] Conditions and regulatory reporting are owned

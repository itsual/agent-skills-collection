# Ethics and Conduct

## Overview

Ethics and conduct programs set behavioral expectations beyond pure legal minima. They protect culture, customers, and the license to operate.

## When to Use

- Code of conduct design and refresh
- Conflicts of interest and gifts/hospitality processes
- Whistleblowing / speak-up channel design
- Conduct risk assessments in customer-facing businesses

## Core Practices

- State expectations in concrete, role-relevant terms
- Provide safe, accessible speak-up channels
- Prohibit retaliation and monitor for it
- Manage conflicts of interest with disclosure and mitigation
- Investigate concerns proportionately and document outcomes
- Report thematic conduct issues to governance

## Principles

- Culture is what leaders tolerate, not what posters say
- Speak-up fails if people fear career harm
- Conflicts are normal; undisclosed conflicts are the problem
- Fair process builds legitimacy of outcomes

## Verification

- [ ] Code and key policies are accessible and acknowledged
- [ ] Speak-up channels are known and monitored
- [ ] Conflicts disclosure process operates in practice

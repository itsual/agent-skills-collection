# SOX and Financial Controls

## Overview

SOX-style regimes require reliable internal control over financial reporting. Compliance means controls are designed well, operate effectively, and deficiencies are fixed transparently.

## When to Use

- ICFR / SOX program operations
- Control design for financial processes
- Deficiency evaluation and remediation
- Management certification support

## Core Practices

- Identify key controls for material financial processes
- Document risk → control → evidence linkage
- Test design and operating effectiveness on a plan
- Classify deficiencies (deficiency, significant deficiency, material weakness)
- Remediate with owners, dates, and retesting
- Coordinate with external auditors early on issues

## Principles

- Key controls should be few enough to test well
- Manual detective controls are weaker than automated preventive where feasible
- Late surprise deficiencies destroy certification confidence
- Tone at the top influences control culture

## Verification

- [ ] Key controls inventory is current
- [ ] Testing results and exceptions are tracked
- [ ] Open deficiencies have remediation plans

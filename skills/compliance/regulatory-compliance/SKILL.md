# Regulatory Compliance

## Overview

Regulatory compliance turns external rules into internal obligations, controls, and proof. Effective programs are risk-based, owned, and evidence-ready — not binder theater.

## When to Use

- Building or maturing a compliance management system
- Mapping products and processes to applicable regulations
- Preparing for examinations or supervisory reviews
- Clarifying ownership between legal, compliance, risk, and business

## Core Practices

- Inventory applicable laws, rules, and guidance by product/jurisdiction
- Translate obligations into control requirements and policies
- Assign accountable owners for each material obligation
- Maintain evidence of design and operating effectiveness
- Track issues, breaches, and remediation to closure
- Report compliance posture to governance forums

## Principles

- Know which rules actually apply — scope before heroics
- Compliance is a business control system, not only a legal opinion
- Evidence must be retrievable under exam pressure
- Proportionate effort: material risk gets deeper controls

## Verification

- [ ] Obligation inventory is current and scoped
- [ ] Material obligations have owners and controls
- [ ] Evidence and issue tracking are operational

# Operational Resilience Compliance

## Overview

Operational resilience rules require firms to prevent, adapt, respond to, and recover from disruptions to important business services — within defined impact tolerances.

## When to Use

- Identifying important/critical business services
- Setting impact tolerances
- Mapping dependencies (people, tech, facilities, third parties)
- Scenario testing and remediation of vulnerabilities

## Core Practices

- Define important business services through a customer/market lens
- Set impact tolerances for disruption (time, volume, metrics)
- Map end-to-end resources and third-party dependencies
- Run severe but plausible scenario tests
- Remediate vulnerabilities that breach tolerances
- Report resilience posture to governance and regulators as required

## Principles

- Resilience is outcome-based, not only uptime of individual systems
- Third parties are inside the resilience boundary for critical services
- Testing must be honest about failure modes
- Investment should follow tolerance breaches, not only audit aesthetics

## Verification

- [ ] Important services and tolerances are approved
- [ ] Dependency maps are maintained
- [ ] Scenario tests drive remediation actions

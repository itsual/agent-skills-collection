# Regulatory Exam Management

## Overview

Exam management is a controlled process: prepare evidence, respond accurately, manage fieldwork, and close findings with credible remediation — without improvisation under pressure.

## When to Use

- Scheduled regulatory examinations
- Thematic reviews and supervisory requests
- Continuous monitoring engagement with regulators
- Post-exam remediation programs

## Core Practices

- Maintain exam-ready evidence for high-risk areas year-round
- Centralize request tracking (PBC-style) with owners and due dates
- Ensure responses are accurate, consistent, and version-controlled
- Brief SMEs before interviews; debrief after
- Track findings, management responses, and remediation to validation
- Capture lessons learned into BAU controls

## Principles

- Surprises during exams usually reflect weak BAU evidence discipline
- Never guess in written responses — verify
- Tone is professional and factual, not defensive theater
- Remediation credibility is judged by delivery, not promises

- [ ] Request log and response QA process exist
- [ ] Findings have owners, dates, and validation criteria
- [ ] Recurring exam themes trigger systemic fixes

# Whistleblowing Investigations

## Overview

Whistleblowing channels only work if investigations are trusted. Process quality — confidentiality, impartiality, and protection from retaliation — determines whether people speak up next time.

## When to Use

- Designing investigation playbooks for speak-up reports
- Managing allegations of misconduct, fraud, or regulatory breaches
- Training managers on non-retaliation duties
- Reporting thematic investigation outcomes to governance

## Core Practices

- Acknowledge reports and assess urgency/risk quickly
- Protect identity to the extent possible and lawful
- Assign investigators without conflicts of interest
- Plan scope, evidence collection, and interview approach
- Document findings and proportionate outcomes
- Monitor for retaliation after reports
- Feed systemic issues into control remediation

## Principles

- Presumption of process fairness for all parties
- Retaliation destroys the entire speak-up system
- Not every report is substantiated — and that must be OK
- Regulatory reporting duties may still apply alongside internal process

## Verification

- [ ] Investigation procedure is documented and followed
- [ ] Conflicts are screened before assignment
- [ ] Non-retaliation monitoring is active

# Regulatory Change Management

## Overview

Regulatory change management prevents “we didn’t know” failures. It connects horizon scanning to impact analysis, project delivery, and control updates.

## When to Use

- Horizon scanning for new rules and guidance
- Impact assessment for proposed or final regulations
- Implementation programs ahead of effective dates
- Multi-jurisdiction change coordination

## Core Practices

- Source regulatory updates from reliable channels
- Triage relevance quickly (applies / does not apply / watch)
- Assess impact on products, processes, systems, and data
- Assign implementation owners and milestones
- Update policies, controls, training, and monitoring
- Evidence completion before the effective date

## Principles

- Late interpretation creates emergency projects
- “Wait for final guidance” is not always a safe strategy
- Cross-functional impact (ops, tech, risk) must be explicit
- Change logs support exam narratives

## Verification

- [ ] Regulatory pipeline is actively maintained
- [ ] Material changes have impact assessments and owners
- [ ] Implementation status is reported to governance

# Records Management

## Overview

Records management ensures the organization keeps what it must, protects it appropriately, and disposes of what it should — on schedule and with evidence.

## When to Use

- Retention schedule design and rollout
- Exam, litigation, or investigation hold processes
- Reducing over-retention and storage risk
- Migrating systems with historical records

## Core Practices

- Classify record types and map to retention periods
- Define authoritative systems of record
- Apply legal holds that suspend destruction
- Control access and integrity of retained records
- Execute disposition with approval and logging
- Train staff on what is a record vs working material

## Principles

- Keep-everything is not a retention strategy — it is risk
- If you cannot find it, you effectively did not retain it
- Holds override normal disposal — process must be reliable
- Privacy and retention must be designed together

## Verification

- [ ] Retention schedule covers material record types
- [ ] Legal hold process is tested
- [ ] Disposition is authorized and auditable

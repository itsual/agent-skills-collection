# Consumer Protection Compliance

## Overview

Consumer protection rules require firms to treat retail customers fairly — clear information, suitable products, and non-abusive practices across the product lifecycle.

## When to Use

- Retail product design and approval
- Marketing and disclosure reviews
- Sales practice and incentive assessments
- Complaint theme remediation

## Core Practices

- Embed fair-treatment principles in product governance
- Ensure disclosures are clear, timely, and not misleading
- Review fees, default paths, and cancellation friction
- Align sales incentives with customer outcomes
- Monitor vulnerable customer treatment where required
- Use complaints and outcomes data as leading indicators

## Principles

- Technical legal compliance can still fail fairness tests
- Dark patterns and pressure tactics create regulatory and reputational risk
- Outcomes matter — not only process narratives
- Marketing and product teams share accountability with compliance

## Verification

- [ ] Product approval considers consumer harm scenarios
- [ ] Key communications pass clarity/fairness review
- [ ] Complaint themes feed control improvements

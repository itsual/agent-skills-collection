# Compliance Risk Assessment

## Overview

Compliance risk assessment evaluates the likelihood and impact of failing to meet obligations. It focuses resources where regulatory, customer, and reputational harm is greatest.

## When to Use

- Annual or product-launch compliance risk assessments
- Prioritizing monitoring and testing plans
- New product / new jurisdiction risk reviews
- Board or risk committee compliance reporting

## Core Practices

- Identify obligations and inherent risk by process/product
- Evaluate control design and operating strength
- Rate residual risk with consistent scales
- Document rationale and key assumptions
- Link high residual risk to action plans and KRIs
- Refresh when regulations, products, or incidents change the profile

## Principles

- Inherent risk without control context is incomplete
- Not every red item can be #1 — force rank
- Assessments should drive resource allocation
- Optimism bias is a common failure mode — challenge it

## Verification

- [ ] Methodology is consistent and documented
- [ ] High risks have remediation or acceptance decisions
- [ ] Results inform monitoring plans

# Compliance Training

## Overview

Compliance training builds awareness and capability to follow requirements. Effective training is targeted, timely, and measured — not generic annual noise.

## When to Use

- Mandatory enterprise compliance curricula
- Role-based training (e.g. AML for ops, privacy for engineers)
- Training after incidents or control failures
- New-hire and transfer compliance onboarding

## Core Practices

- Map training to risks and roles, not only to a single course list
- Use realistic scenarios over pure legal text
- Track assignment, completion, and overdue escalation
- Refresh when rules or products change materially
- Test comprehension where risk warrants it
- Measure whether incidents/errors decline in trained areas

## Principles

- Relevance drives retention
- Completion ≠ competence — but non-completion is still a control failure
- Micro-learning beats rare marathon modules for many topics
- Leadership visible support improves take-up

## Verification

- [ ] Role-based curricula exist for high-risk roles
- [ ] Completion is monitored and escalated
- [ ] Content is current with material rule changes

# Data Stewardship

## Overview

Data stewards are the operational caretakers of data domains. They maintain definitions, triage quality issues, and help consumers use data correctly.

## When to Use

- Implementing domain-oriented governance
- Reducing bottleneck dependence on a central data team for every definition fight
- Improving responsiveness to quality incidents
- Training business-side data champions

## Core Practices

- Clarify steward responsibilities vs data owner accountability
- Maintain business definitions and approved calculations
- Triage and route quality issues
- Approve or challenge changes to critical fields
- Support consumers with “how to use this data” guidance
- Escalate unresolved cross-domain conflicts to governance forums

## Principles

- Stewardship is ongoing work — budget time for it
- Stewards need authority proportional to responsibility
- Best stewards combine domain knowledge with data literacy
- Central teams enable stewards; they should not replace them for every domain

## Verification

- [ ] Stewards are named for critical domains
- [ ] Issue response expectations are defined
- [ ] Definitions are maintained and discoverable

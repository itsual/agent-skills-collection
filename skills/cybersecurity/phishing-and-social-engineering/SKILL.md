# Phishing and Social Engineering

## Overview

Social engineering targets people and processes. Effective defense combines email/web controls, easy reporting, rapid response, and practice that builds habits without blame.

## When to Use

- Email security and anti-phishing controls
- Awareness programs and simulations
- Business email compromise (BEC) controls
- Improving report-and-respond workflows

## Core Practices

- Deploy technical email protections (filtering, authentication: SPF/DKIM/DMARC)
- Make reporting suspicious messages one-click easy
- Respond quickly to reports (contain, reset, communicate)
- Run simulations that teach, not only punish
- Add process controls for high-risk actions (wire changes, data export)
- Address vishing and non-email channels too

## Principles

- Anyone can be phished — culture should reward reporting
- Shame-based programs drive under-reporting
- High-value processes need step-up verification beyond email trust
- Technical controls buy down volume; humans remain part of defense

## Verification

- [ ] Email authentication and filtering are in place
- [ ] Reporting path is known and used
- [ ] High-risk business processes have verification steps

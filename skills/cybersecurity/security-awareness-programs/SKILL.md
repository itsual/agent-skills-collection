# Security Awareness Programs

## Overview

Awareness programs reduce human-targeted risk when they are practical, continuous, and respectful. Completion metrics alone do not equal reduced incidents.

## When to Use

- Annual and continuous awareness curricula
- Role-based training (developers, finance, executives, admins)
- Phishing simulation programs
- Security culture campaigns

## Core Practices

- Target behaviors tied to real incident patterns
- Tailor content by role and risk
- Use short, frequent reinforcement over rare marathon modules
- Measure reporting rates, click trends, and incident themes — not only completions
- Avoid shame; reward positive security behaviors
- Update content as threats and tools change

## Principles

- Fear without agency produces learned helplessness
- Executives need awareness too — they are high-value targets
- Awareness complements but does not replace technical controls
- Culture is shaped by what leaders do under pressure

## Verification

- [ ] Content maps to actual organizational risks
- [ ] Role-based paths exist for high-risk roles
- [ ] Behavioral metrics are reviewed, not only completion

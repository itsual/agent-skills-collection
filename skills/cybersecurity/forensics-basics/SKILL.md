# Forensics Basics

## Overview

Digital forensics preserves and analyzes evidence to understand what happened. Mishandling evidence can destroy investigative options and legal usability.

## When to Use

- Security incident investigations requiring depth
- Insider investigations with evidentiary needs
- Malware and host compromise analysis support
- Legal hold and chain-of-custody scenarios

## Core Practices

- Preserve volatile data carefully when needed; image systems appropriately
- Document chain of custody for evidence handling
- Collect logs, disk, memory, and network artifacts per investigation goals
- Analyze with known-good tools and verified methods
- Separate facts from hypotheses in reporting
- Engage specialists for advanced malware or legal-critical cases

## Principles

- Live response can alter evidence — plan intentionally
- Notes and timestamps are part of the investigation product
- Scope creep without hypotheses wastes forensic time
- Legal requirements may dictate handling — know when to escalate

## Verification

- [ ] Evidence handling steps are documented
- [ ] Collection matches investigation questions
- [ ] Reports distinguish observed facts from inference

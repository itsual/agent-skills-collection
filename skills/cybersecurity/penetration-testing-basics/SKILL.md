# Penetration Testing Basics

## Overview

Penetration testing simulates attacker techniques to find exploitable weaknesses. Value comes from clear scope, skilled testing, and disciplined remediation — not the PDF alone.

## When to Use

- Pre-launch or periodic offensive assessments
- Validating critical app or network controls
- Meeting customer/regulatory assessment expectations
- Retesting after major remediation

## Core Practices

- Define scope, rules of engagement, and success criteria in writing
- Ensure testers have enough access and information for the test type
- Prioritize findings by exploitability and business impact
- Assign remediation owners and due dates
- Retest critical findings to verify fixes
- Feed patterns into secure design and developer training

## Principles

- A pen test is a sampling exercise, not a proof of absence of vulns
- Findings without remediation tracking waste money
- Production testing needs careful safety controls
- Internal purple-team learning multiplies one-off test value

## Verification

- [ ] Scope and RoE are documented
- [ ] Critical findings have verified remediation
- [ ] Lessons influence preventive controls

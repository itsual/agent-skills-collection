# Third-Party Security

## Overview

Vendors extend your attack surface. Third-party security risk management evaluates and monitors those who process your data or connect to your systems.

## When to Use

- Vendor security assessments and questionnaires
- SaaS and processor onboarding
- Contractual security schedule negotiation
- Periodic reassessment of critical vendors

## Core Practices

- Tier vendors by data sensitivity and system criticality
- Perform proportionate diligence (questionnaires, SOC reports, testing rights)
- Require contractual security, incident notice, and audit rights as appropriate
- Limit vendor access to least privilege; monitor connections
- Reassess on cadence and on major incidents/changes
- Revoke access promptly at contract end

## Principles

- A questionnaire is not continuous assurance
- Critical vendors need deeper oversight than long-tail tools
- Shared responsibility must be explicit in contracts and architecture
- Fourth parties (subprocessors) can inherit risk

## Verification

- [ ] Critical vendors are tiered and assessed
- [ ] Contracts match risk tier requirements
- [ ] Access is reviewed and revoked on exit

# Tabletop Exercises

## Overview

Tabletops simulate incidents in discussion form to reveal gaps in plans, ownership, and communication before a real crisis.

## When to Use

- IR plan validation
- Ransomware, insider, or third-party breach scenarios
- Executive and board cyber readiness practice
- After major process or org changes

## Core Practices

- Set clear objectives (what decisions/capabilities to test)
- Build realistic injects timed through the session
- Include the real roles who would be involved
- Facilitate neutrally; capture decisions and uncertainties
- End with concrete improvement actions and owners
- Increase complexity over successive exercises

## Principles

- Psychological safety improves honest discovery of gaps
- A tabletop that only congratulates readiness failed its job
- Documentation of decisions is as valuable as the discussion
- Frequency beats perfection of a single annual event

## Verification

- [ ] Objectives and scenario are defined in advance
- [ ] Right stakeholders participate
- [ ] Action items are tracked to closure

# Business Case

## Overview

A business case exists to support a decision. It should make the recommended option and its rationale obvious to the people who control resources.

## When to Use

- Requesting budget or headcount
- Proposing new products, platforms, or major projects
- Build vs buy vs partner decisions
- Strategic initiatives that need formal approval

## Recommended Structure

1. Executive summary & recommendation
2. Problem / opportunity statement
3. Strategic alignment
4. Options considered (including “do nothing”)
5. Recommended option – scope, approach, timeline
6. Costs and benefits (quantitative + qualitative)
7. Risks and mitigations
8. Implementation outline and success metrics
9. Ask / decision required

## Principles

- Lead with the recommendation
- Make assumptions explicit
- Compare options fairly
- Quantify where credible; be honest where not
- Keep the main body concise; put detailed models in appendices

## Verification

- [ ] A decision-maker can understand the recommendation and key trade-offs quickly
- [ ] Costs, benefits, and risks are clearly stated
- [ ] Success metrics are defined

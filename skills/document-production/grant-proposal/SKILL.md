# Grant Proposal

## Overview

Successful grant proposals make it easy for reviewers to see fit, credibility, and impact. They follow the funder’s structure while telling a coherent story.

## When to Use

- Research or academic grant applications
- Nonprofit / foundation funding proposals
- Government or institutional grant programs
- Innovation or social-impact funding calls

## Typical Sections

1. Executive summary / abstract
2. Problem statement and significance
3. Goals and objectives
4. Methods / approach
5. Expected outcomes and impact
6. Evaluation plan
7. Team and organizational capacity
8. Budget and justification
9. Timeline
10. Supporting materials (as required)

## Principles

- Mirror the funder’s language and priorities
- Be specific about outcomes and how they will be measured
- Make the “why this team / why now” obvious
- Follow page limits and formatting rules exactly
- Support claims with evidence and realistic budgets

## Verification

- [ ] Proposal directly addresses the funder’s stated criteria
- [ ] Objectives are specific and measurable
- [ ] Budget aligns with the described work
- [ ] All required sections and attachments are present

# Research Paper

## Overview

A research paper presents a clear question or claim, situates it in existing work, and supports conclusions with evidence and transparent reasoning.

## When to Use

- Academic or scientific papers
- Formal research reports
- Literature reviews
- Technical whitepapers with scholarly structure

## Common Structure (IMRaD-inspired)

1. Title and abstract
2. Introduction (problem, motivation, contribution)
3. Related work / background
4. Methods or approach
5. Results / findings
6. Discussion
7. Conclusion and future work
8. References

## Principles

- State the contribution early and clearly
- Distinguish evidence from interpretation
- Cite sources accurately and consistently
- Make methods reproducible enough for the context
- Use precise language; avoid overclaiming
- Follow the target venue’s style and structure when applicable

## Verification

- [ ] Research question or claim is explicit
- [ ] Related work is fairly represented
- [ ] Conclusions are supported by the presented evidence
- [ ] Citations and structure meet expected standards

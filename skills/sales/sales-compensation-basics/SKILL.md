# Sales Compensation Basics

## Overview

Sales compensation steers behavior. Good plans are understood in minutes, pay for the outcomes the business needs, and avoid loopholes that reward the wrong things.

## When to Use

- Designing annual comp plans
- Adjusting incentives for new motions or products
- Diagnosing sandbagging, discounting, or neglect of renewals
- Onboarding reps onto plan mechanics

## Core Practices

- Align metrics to strategy (new logo, expansion, retention mix)
- Keep the plan explainable on one page
- Set quotas from capacity and historical reality
- Balance base and variable appropriately for the role
- Include guardrails against harmful discounting if needed
- Model earnings at threshold, target, and excellence

## Principles

- You get what you pay for — literally
- Complexity creates confusion and distrust
- Changing plans mid-year damages morale unless handled carefully
- Comp cannot fix a broken product or ICP

## Verification

- [ ] Reps can explain how they earn on the plan
- [ ] Incentives match strategic priorities
- [ ] Plan is modeled for cost and motivation outcomes

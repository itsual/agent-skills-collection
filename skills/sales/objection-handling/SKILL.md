# Objection Handling

## Overview

Objections are information. Skilled handling clarifies the real concern, addresses it honestly, and either advances the deal or surfaces a true blocker early.

## When to Use

- Price and budget pushback
- “We’re happy with the status quo / competitor”
- Timing and priority objections
- Security, legal, or implementation concerns
- Authority and stakeholder objections

## Core Practices

- Listen fully; don’t interrupt with a rebuttal
- Clarify what the objection really means
- Acknowledge the legitimacy of the concern
- Respond with evidence, options, or reframing
- Confirm whether the concern is resolved
- Know when to walk away

## Principles

- Most objections are either misunderstandings, risk signals, or negotiation moves
- Honesty about limitations builds more trust than spin
- Price objections often hide value gaps from weak discovery
- Document recurring objections to improve product and enablement

## Verification

- [ ] The real concern was clarified, not assumed
- [ ] Response used relevant proof or options
- [ ] Outcome (advance, stall, or exit) is explicit

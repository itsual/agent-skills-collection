# Proposal and Negotiation

## Overview

Proposals package the solution, value, and commercial terms. Negotiation is the process of reaching agreement without giving away margin or setting bad precedents.

## When to Use

- Formal proposals and statements of work
- Pricing and packaging negotiations
- Contract term discussions
- Multi-year and expansion deals

## Core Practices

- Anchor proposals in the outcomes discovery uncovered
- Present options (good / better / best) when helpful
- Define what’s included and explicitly excluded
- Negotiate trade-offs, not only price cuts
- Know your walk-away and approval thresholds
- Document agreements clearly to avoid implementation friction

## Principles

- Discount without trade-offs trains buyers to wait you out
- Value justification should precede price discussion
- Procurement tactics are normal — prepare for them
- Long-term relationship value matters, but so does precedent

## Verification

- [ ] Proposal ties to agreed outcomes and scope
- [ ] Commercial terms are clear and approved
- [ ] Trade-offs are explicit when price moves

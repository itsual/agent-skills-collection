# Sales Enablement

## Overview

Sales enablement ensures reps can execute the sales strategy. It covers onboarding, continuous training, content, tools, and feedback loops between sales and the rest of GTM.

## When to Use

- Onboarding new reps
- Rolling out new products, pricing, or plays
- Improving win rates and cycle times
- Creating battle cards, talk tracks, and demo scripts

## Core Practices

- Map enablement to the buyer journey and sales stages
- Prioritize the few assets reps actually use
- Train on skills (discovery, negotiation) not only slides
- Measure adoption and impact on performance
- Close the loop from field feedback to content updates
- Partner with product marketing and sales leadership

## Principles

- Enablement is ineffective if content is unfindable or outdated
- Practice and coaching beat passive consumption
- One great playbook beats twenty ignored PDFs
- Field input is the quality control system

## Verification

- [ ] Reps can find and use critical assets quickly
- [ ] Training ties to measurable sales behaviors
- [ ] Content is current and owned

# Sales Strategy

## Overview

Sales strategy decides who you sell to, how you sell, and with what resources. It connects ICP, motion design, capacity planning, and revenue goals into an executable system.

## When to Use

- Designing or redesigning sales motions
- Choosing self-serve vs sales-assisted vs enterprise paths
- Planning headcount, territories, and quotas
- Aligning sales with product packaging and marketing demand

## Core Elements

- ICP and segment prioritization
- Motion design (inbound, outbound, PLG-assist, partner)
- Average deal size, cycle length, and win rates by segment
- Capacity model and quota design
- Coverage of the funnel from lead to close
- Feedback loops to product and marketing

## Principles

- Strategy should match how buyers actually buy
- Don’t force enterprise process onto transactional deals (or vice versa)
- Capacity math must be honest about ramp and attainment
- Sales strategy without ICP clarity becomes spray-and-pray

## Verification

- [ ] Target segments and motions are explicit
- [ ] Economics (ACV, cycle, win rate) underpin the plan
- [ ] Sales, marketing, and product share definitions of success

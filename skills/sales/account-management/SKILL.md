# Account Management

## Overview

Account management protects and grows revenue after the initial sale. It combines relationship health, adoption, commercial timing, and multi-threaded coverage of the account.

## When to Use

- Renewal and expansion ownership
- Strategic / key account planning
- QBR and executive business reviews
- Risk intervention before churn

## Core Practices

- Maintain an account plan: goals, stakeholders, risks, opportunities
- Track product adoption and outcome realization
- Multi-thread relationships beyond a single champion
- Run structured business reviews tied to value
- Coordinate with CS, support, and product on risk signals
- Build expansion theses grounded in usage and business change

## Principles

- Renewal starts the day after close
- Value evidence beats last-minute discounting
- Single-threaded accounts are retention risks
- Expansion is earned through success, not pressure alone

## Verification

- [ ] Account plans exist for priority accounts
- [ ] Risk signals have owners and actions
- [ ] Expansion opportunities are evidence-based

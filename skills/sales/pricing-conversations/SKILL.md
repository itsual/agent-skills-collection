# Pricing Conversations

## Overview

Price conversations go wrong when value isn’t established first or when discounts are given without trade-offs. Strong pricing dialogue connects cost to outcomes and commercial structure.

## When to Use

- Initial price presentation
- Discount and procurement pressure
- Packaging and tier selection
- Multi-year and expansion pricing

## Core Practices

- Establish value and impact before numbers when possible
- Present price in the context of outcomes and alternatives
- Offer structured options rather than open-ended discounting
- Trade for term, volume, scope, or case study rights when reducing price
- Know approval thresholds and walk-away points
- Document commercial rationale for internal consistency

## Principles

- Unanchored discounts train buyers to wait
- Silence after stating price is a tool, not awkwardness to fill
- Procurement’s job is to reduce price; your job is to defend value
- Clarity on what’s included prevents downstream scope fights

## Verification

- [ ] Value context precedes or accompanies price
- [ ] Discount (if any) has a corresponding trade
- [ ] Final terms are unambiguous

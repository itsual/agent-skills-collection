# Inbound Qualification

## Overview

Inbound interest varies widely in quality. Good qualification protects AE time, improves conversion rates, and gives marketing feedback on lead quality.

## When to Use

- SDR/AE handoff design
- Implementing BANT, MEDDICC, or custom qualification
- Improving speed-to-lead and SLA compliance
- Reducing junk pipeline from unqualified leads

## Core Practices

- Define qualification criteria with sales and marketing together
- Respond fast to high-intent inbound
- Ask enough to judge fit without turning the call into interrogation
- Route by segment, territory, or product interest
- Capture disqualification reasons for marketing feedback
- Hand off with context, not just a CRM stage change

## Principles

- Speed-to-lead is a competitive advantage
- Shared definitions prevent “marketing says qualified, sales says not”
- Disqualification is a success when it saves time
- Feedback loops improve top-of-funnel quality over time

## Verification

- [ ] Qualification criteria are written and agreed
- [ ] SLA for response is defined and measured
- [ ] Handoffs include context the AE needs

# Demo and Presentation

## Overview

A great demo is a story about the customer’s success, not a feature tour. It connects discovery insights to proof that your product can deliver the outcome they care about.

## When to Use

- Product demos after discovery
- Executive presentations and business reviews
- Proof-of-concept kickoffs
- Sales engineering collaboration

## Core Practices

- Recap discovery and confirm priorities before showing anything
- Structure around 2–3 critical use cases, not the full product
- Show workflows with realistic data and their language
- Pause for reactions; don’t monologue through clicks
- Handle “can it do X?” without derailing the narrative
- End with clear next steps and success criteria

## Principles

- Relevance > completeness
- Tell the story of before → after with their context
- Live demos need backup plans (recordings, environments)
- SE and AE should rehearse roles

## Verification

- [ ] Demo maps to stated pains from discovery
- [ ] Prospect engages with questions and reactions
- [ ] Next step is agreed before the call ends

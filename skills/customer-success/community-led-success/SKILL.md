# Community-Led Success

## Overview

Community-led success harnesses peers to help peers. It scales knowledge, surfaces product insight, and strengthens belonging among customers.

## When to Use

- Launching or maturing a user community
- Deflecting repetitive how-to demand
- Developing champions and power users
- Gathering grassroots product feedback

## Core Practices

- Define community purpose beyond “marketing channel”
- Seed expert content and moderate for quality
- Recognize and elevate helpful members
- Connect community insights to CS and Product
- Measure health: active members, solved questions, sentiment
- Integrate community into onboarding and education paths

## Principles

- Community is reciprocal — extractive programs die
- Moderation and psychological safety are product features
- Not every segment wants community equally
- Peer answers can be more trusted than vendor answers

## Verification

- [ ] Purpose and success metrics are defined
- [ ] Moderation and recognition systems operate
- [ ] Insights flow to Product/CS on a cadence

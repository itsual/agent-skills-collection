# Journey Orchestration (Post-Sale)

## Overview

Journey orchestration aligns timing, channels, and owners across the customer lifecycle so each stage prepares the next — without conflicting messages or gaps.

## When to Use

- Mapping the full post-sale journey
- Reducing handoff gaps between teams
- Designing stage transitions (onboarding → adoption → renew)
- Unifying digital and human touchpoints

## Core Practices

- Define stages, entry/exit criteria, and success metrics
- Map touchpoints by stage, channel, and owner
- Remove duplicate or conflicting outreach
- Trigger stage transitions from signals (not only time)
- Align content and plays to stage goals
- Measure stage conversion and drop-off reasons

## Principles

- The customer experiences one journey; org charts are invisible to them
- Time-based journeys underperform signal-based ones
- Orchestration requires shared data and definitions
- Less coordinated noise often beats more activity

## Verification

- [ ] Stages and criteria are documented
- [ ] Owners exist for each major touchpoint class
- [ ] Drop-offs between stages are measured

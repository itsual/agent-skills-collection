# Expansion Playbooks

## Overview

Expansion works when customers are successful and see a logical next step. Playbooks codify when to expand, who to involve, and how to prove incremental value.

## When to Use

- Designing CS-assisted expansion motions
- Identifying expansion signals in product and conversations
- Coordinating CS qualification with Account Executives
- Packaging land-and-expand paths by segment

## Core Practices

- Define expansion triggers (usage ceilings, new use cases, org growth)
- Qualify expansion opportunities with customer outcomes in mind
- Map plays: seat growth, module attach, new department, higher tier
- Equip CS with talk tracks and proof points; know when to bring Sales
- Track expansion pipeline sourced from CS signals
- Avoid pushing expansion into unhealthy accounts

## Principles

- Expansion follows value; forced expansion accelerates churn
- CS credibility is an asset — don’t spend it on bad fits
- Clear CS→Sales handoff rules prevent conflict and drops
- Product-qualified expansion signals beat gut feel alone

## Verification

- [ ] Plays and triggers are documented
- [ ] Handoff criteria to Sales are clear
- [ ] Expansion outcomes are measured by play

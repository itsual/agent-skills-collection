# Professional Services Alignment

## Overview

PS delivers projects; CS owns ongoing outcomes. Misalignment creates “successful go-live, failed adoption” patterns. Joint process fixes that gap.

## When to Use

- Implementation-to-CSM handoff design
- Complex enterprise deployments
- Scoping PS vs CS responsibilities
- Reducing post-go-live surprises

## Core Practices

- Define exit criteria from PS that include adoption readiness, not only technical delivery
- Involve CS early on strategic implementations
- Transfer context: stakeholders, risks, unresolved items, success metrics
- Align commercial expectations (what was sold vs what was delivered)
- Joint retrospectives on troubled launches
- Feedback loop from CS into PS packages and estimates

## Principles

- Go-live ≠ value realized
- Early CS involvement beats cold handoffs
- Scope gaps sold by Sales hurt both PS and CS
- Shared definitions of “done” prevent ownership fights

## Verification

- [ ] Handoff checklist includes adoption and stakeholder context
- [ ] CS is engaged before go-live on strategic accounts
- [ ] Post-launch issues inform PS packaging

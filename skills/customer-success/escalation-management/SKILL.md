# Escalation Management

## Overview

Escalations are moments of truth. Good escalation management restores confidence through ownership, honest communication, and visible progress — not silence or blame.

## When to Use

- Severity-1 product or service incidents
- Executive-to-executive customer complaints
- Multi-team blockers threatening renewal
- Defining escalation paths and war-room norms

## Core Practices

- Define severity levels and escalation criteria
- Appoint a single accountable owner for the customer relationship during the event
- Set communication cadence with the customer (even if “no update yet”)
- Coordinate technical, support, and leadership streams internally
- Document timeline, impact, and resolution
- Run post-escalation review with preventive actions

## Principles

- Speed of acknowledgment matters as much as speed of fix
- Over-communicate early; under-communicate destroys trust
- Don’t promise dates you can’t defend
- Learning after the event is part of the process

## Verification

- [ ] Severity and path definitions exist
- [ ] Customers know who owns their escalation
- [ ] Post-mortems produce tracked actions

# Success Planning

## Overview

A success plan is a shared roadmap for what the customer will achieve with the product and how both sides will get there. It anchors CS activity to outcomes.

## When to Use

- Strategic and enterprise account management
- Post-onboarding steady-state engagement
- Aligning multiple stakeholders on priorities
- Creating accountability for adoption initiatives

## Core Practices

- Co-define goals in the customer’s business language
- Set measurable outcomes and timeboxes
- Assign mutual owners and dependencies
- Review progress on a cadence; adjust openly
- Connect plan milestones to renewal and expansion timing
- Keep plans living documents, not shelfware

## Principles

- Customer goals > internal activity checklists
- Short, focused plans beat 30-page aspiration decks
- Plans without executive sponsorship struggle
- Update when strategy or stakeholders change

## Verification

- [ ] Goals are customer-owned and measurable
- [ ] Owners and dates exist for key milestones
- [ ] Plan is reviewed on an agreed cadence

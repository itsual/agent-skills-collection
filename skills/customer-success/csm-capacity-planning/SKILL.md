# CSM Capacity Planning

## Overview

Capacity planning prevents both neglect (too many accounts per CSM) and inefficiency (too few). It links segment strategy to headcount and workload design.

## When to Use

- Setting or revising CSM:customer ratios
- Hiring plans and territory design
- Rebalancing books of business
- Assessing burnout and coverage risk

## Core Practices

- Estimate effort by segment (onboarding load, touch frequency, complexity)
- Model book size ranges, not single magic ratios
- Account for ramp time for new CSMs
- Include non-account work (projects, escalations, enablement)
- Rebalance when portfolio mix shifts
- Track leading overload signals (overdue tasks, health neglect)

## Principles

- ACV alone is a weak proxy for effort
- Onboarding spikes break average-based models
- Sustainable capacity beats heroic coverage stories
- Digital motions change the capacity equation

## Verification

- [ ] Effort assumptions by segment are explicit
- [ ] Hiring/coverage plans use those assumptions
- [ ] Overload indicators are monitored

# Customer Success Strategy

## Overview

CS strategy decides which customers get which level of help, what “success” means, and how CS creates retention and expansion value without becoming unscalable concierge service.

## When to Use

- Building or restructuring a CS function
- Choosing high-touch vs tech-touch vs hybrid coverage
- Aligning CS goals with revenue retention targets
- Defining the CS role versus Support, Sales, and Product

## Core Practices

- Segment customers by value, potential, and needs
- Match coverage model (1:1, pooled, digital) to segments
- Define success outcomes per segment (not only activity metrics)
- Set clear handoffs with Sales, Support, and Professional Services
- Align CS capacity to book-of-business reality
- Measure leading indicators of retention and expansion

## Principles

- Not every customer needs a dedicated CSM
- Success is customer outcomes, not email volume
- CS strategy must match product complexity and ACV
- Scale requires product-led success paths, not only headcount

## Verification

- [ ] Segments and coverage model are explicit
- [ ] CS outcomes tie to retention/expansion goals
- [ ] Role boundaries with adjacent teams are clear

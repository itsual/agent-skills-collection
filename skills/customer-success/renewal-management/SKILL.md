# Renewal Management

## Overview

Renewals should not be a surprise 30 days before expiry. Managed renewals start early, multi-thread stakeholders, and lead with value delivered.

## When to Use

- Renewal pipeline and forecasting
- At-risk renewal intervention
- Aligning CS and commercial owners on renewals
- Multi-year and price-increase renewals

## Core Practices

- Start renewal prep on a defined lead-time by segment
- Confirm decision process, budget owner, and timeline
- Assemble value evidence (outcomes, adoption, ROI stories)
- Surface and resolve risks well before the date
- Coordinate commercial terms with Sales/AM as appropriate
- Capture win/loss reasons on every renewal outcome

## Principles

- Renewal risk is often visible months earlier in health/adoption
- Single-threaded renewals are fragile
- Discounting is not a substitute for unresolved value gaps
- Forecast honesty beats optimistic renewal theater

## Verification

- [ ] Renewal calendar and owners exist
- [ ] At-risk renewals have intervention plans
- [ ] Outcomes and reasons are logged

# Cohort and Retention Finance

## Overview

Cohort analysis tracks how groups of customers acquired in a period behave over time. It reveals whether the business keeps and expands customers — the core of durable value.

## When to Use

- LTV estimation
- Churn diagnosis
- Channel quality comparison
- Board-level retention reporting

## Core Practices

- Define cohort basis (signup month, first purchase, first paid)
- Track retention of logos and revenue separately when useful
- Include expansion in net retention views
- Compare cohorts to isolate product/channel changes
- Link retention curves to LTV and payback models

## Principles

- Blended averages hide cohort deterioration
- Early cohort months are leading indicators
- Retention is a product and CS outcome with finance consequences
- Methodology consistency enables learning

## Verification

- [ ] Cohort definition is explicit
- [ ] Curves are comparable across periods
- [ ] Insights feed product, CS, and growth decisions

# Revenue Recognition

## Overview

Revenue recognition determines when sales become revenue. Getting it wrong creates restatements, investor distrust, and broken incentives.

## When to Use

- New pricing or contract models
- SaaS, multi-element, and long-term contracts
- Audit readiness and policy documentation
- Deal desk reviews with non-standard terms

## Core Practices

- Identify contracts and performance obligations
- Determine transaction price (including variable consideration)
- Allocate price to obligations
- Recognize as obligations are satisfied (point in time or over time)
- Document policies and exceptions

## Principles

- Cash collected ≠ revenue earned
- Side agreements and unusual terms are red flags
- Systems must support the policy, not only the invoice
- Conservative, consistent application builds credibility

## Verification

- [ ] Policy matches business model and standards framework
- [ ] Non-standard deals get review
- [ ] Deferred revenue and contract assets are tracked

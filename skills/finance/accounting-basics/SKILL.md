# Accounting Basics

## Overview

Accounting records economic activity so stakeholders can understand performance and position. Basics done well prevent costly cleanup and bad decisions later.

## When to Use

- Setting up or restructuring books
- Training operators on financial literacy
- Diagnosing messy ledgers
- Bridging operational events to journal entries

## Core Concepts

- Double-entry (debits and credits)
- Accrual vs cash basis
- Matching principle
- Chart of accounts design
- Assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, expenses

## Principles

- Every transaction has two sides
- Period cut-off discipline prevents distorted results
- Chart of accounts should match how the business is managed
- Documentation supports audit and trust

## Verification

- [ ] Trial balance concepts are understood
- [ ] Accruals and cut-off are applied consciously
- [ ] Account structure supports reporting needs

# Treasury Operations

## Overview

Treasury ensures the company can pay its obligations, deploy excess cash prudently, and control payment fraud and operational risk.

## When to Use

- Daily/weekly cash positioning
- Bank account structure design
- Payment factory and controls
- Short-term investment policy

## Core Practices

- Consolidate cash visibility across entities and banks
- Optimize account structures and pooling where appropriate
- Enforce payment authorization and dual controls
- Forecast short-term cash with treasury ownership
- Define investment policy for surplus cash (safety, liquidity, yield)

## Principles

- Capital preservation and liquidity first for operating cash
- Fraud controls are non-negotiable
- Idle cash in fragmented accounts is a silent cost
- Segregation of duties protects the company

## Verification

- [ ] Cash positions are visible and timely
- [ ] Payment controls are enforced
- [ ] Investment policy matches risk tolerance

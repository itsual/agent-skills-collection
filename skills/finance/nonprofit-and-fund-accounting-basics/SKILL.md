# Nonprofit and Fund Accounting Basics

## Overview

Fund accounting tracks resources by restriction and purpose. It ensures donor-restricted funds are used and reported correctly.

## When to Use

- Nonprofit financial management
- Grant accounting and compliance
- Restricted vs unrestricted net assets
- Board reporting for mission organizations

## Core Concepts

- Without-donor-restriction vs with-donor-restriction net assets
- Release from restrictions
- Grant budgets, allowability, and reporting
- Functional expense allocation (program, admin, fundraising)

## Principles

- Restrictions are legal and ethical obligations
- Commingling restricted funds operationally requires careful tracking
- Overhead allocation policies must be defensible
- Transparency supports donor trust

## Verification

- [ ] Restricted funds are tracked by purpose
- [ ] Releases are supported by qualifying spend
- [ ] Grant reports reconcile to books

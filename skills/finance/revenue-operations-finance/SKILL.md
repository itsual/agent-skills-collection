# Revenue Operations Finance

## Overview

RevOps and finance meet at bookings, billings, revenue, and forecast. Alignment prevents the classic gap between “sales says we’ll close” and “finance can recognize/collect.”

## When to Use

- Aligning sales and finance forecasts
- Bookings vs billings vs revenue bridges
- GTM efficiency and CAC payback reviews
- Comp plan financial modeling support

## Core Practices

- Agree definitions: pipeline, bookings, ARR, revenue
- Bridge sales forecast to financial forecast explicitly
- Audit quote-to-cash handoffs for leakage
- Measure GTM spend against pipeline and bookings quality
- Partner on deal desk for non-standard terms

## Principles

- Shared definitions kill most finance–sales wars
- Bookings quality predicts revenue quality
- Finance should enable speed with guardrails, not only block
- Attribution arguments need data discipline

## Verification

- [ ] Bookings/revenue definitions are shared
- [ ] Forecast bridge exists and is reviewed
- [ ] Non-standard deals have finance visibility

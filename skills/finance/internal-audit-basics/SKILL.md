# Internal Audit Basics

## Overview

Internal audit provides independent assurance that controls and processes work as intended. Even lean teams benefit from risk-based review discipline.

## When to Use

- Establishing or scoping internal audit
- Reviewing high-risk processes (payments, revenue, access)
- Following up on external audit findings
- Assurance for the board/audit committee

## Core Practices

- Start from risk, not from a generic checklist
- Define scope, criteria, and evidence standards
- Test design and operating effectiveness
- Report findings with severity and actionable remediation
- Track remediation to closure

## Principles

- Independence and objectivity are the product
- Findings without remediation ownership are incomplete
- Partner with the business; don’t only police
- Focus on what could actually hurt the company

## Verification

- [ ] Reviews are risk-prioritized
- [ ] Findings have owners and due dates
- [ ] Follow-up verifies closure, not only promises

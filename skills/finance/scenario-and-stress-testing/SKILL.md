# Scenario and Stress Testing

## Overview

Scenarios explore plausible alternative futures; stress tests push key assumptions to breaking points. Both improve preparedness and capital/liquidity planning.

## When to Use

- Annual planning downside cases
- Liquidity and runway stress analysis
- Covenant and debt capacity under stress
- Strategic contingency planning

## Core Practices

- Define scenarios with coherent storylines (not random shocks only)
- Stress the few variables that actually break the business
- Link operational triggers to financial outcomes
- Pre-define management actions under each scenario
- Present results as decision inputs, not fear theater

## Principles

- Plausibility and usefulness beat extreme fantasy
- Correlation of shocks matters (everything breaks together)
- Actions under stress must be operationally realistic
- Update scenarios when the world changes

## Verification

- [ ] Scenarios are coherent and material
- [ ] Liquidity/solvency impacts are explicit
- [ ] Contingency actions are listed, not implied

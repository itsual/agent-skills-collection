# Board Financial Reporting

## Overview

Board financial reporting informs fiduciary oversight. It must be accurate, comparable, and honest about risks — without burying the lead in detail.

## When to Use

- Quarterly/annual board meetings
- Investor board observers and updates
- Fundraising diligence financial sections
- Audit committee materials

## Core Practices

- Summarize results vs plan and vs prior period
- Present cash, runway, and liquidity explicitly
- Highlight risks, judgments, and one-time items
- Include forward outlook and key assumptions
- Keep appendices for detail; narrative for decisions
- Align with prior board packs for continuity

## Principles

- No surprises for the board on material issues
- Bad news early is a feature of good governance
- Directors need insight, not a data room dump
- Consistency of metrics builds institutional memory

## Verification

- [ ] Material variances and risks are surfaced
- [ ] Cash/liquidity position is unambiguous
- [ ] Pack is reviewable in limited pre-read time

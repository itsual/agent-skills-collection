# Personal Finance for Founders

## Overview

Founders often blur personal and company finances. Clear boundaries, appropriate compensation, and equity literacy protect both the individual and the company.

## When to Use

- Setting founder compensation
- Equity grant and dilution decisions
- Secondary sales and liquidity planning
- Separating personal guarantees and company obligations

## Core Practices

- Pay market-aware but stage-appropriate salaries
- Keep personal and corporate accounts strictly separate
- Understand equity value is illiquid and risky until proven otherwise
- Plan for tax events on equity (exercise, AMT, exits)
- Avoid using the company as a personal piggy bank

## Principles

- Undercapitalized founders make desperate company decisions
- Overpaid founders signal weak governance to investors
- Personal guarantees are real risk transfer
- Professional advice on equity tax is usually worth it

## Verification

- [ ] Compensation is documented and approved appropriately
- [ ] Personal/corporate separation is clean
- [ ] Equity and tax implications are understood before major actions

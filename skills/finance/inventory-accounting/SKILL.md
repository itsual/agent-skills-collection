# Inventory Accounting

## Overview

Inventory is both an operational asset and an accounting judgment area. Valuation method, reserves, and cut-off directly affect gross margin and balance sheet health.

## When to Use

- Inventory valuation and reserves
- Cycle counts and physical inventory
- Gross margin investigations
- Obsolescence and slow-moving stock

## Core Practices

- Apply consistent valuation method (FIFO, weighted average, etc.)
- Assess NRV and obsolescence reserves
- Enforce receiving and shipping cut-off
- Reconcile perpetual records to physical counts
- Partner with ops on slow-moving and excess stock

## Principles

- Optimistic inventory is future margin disappointment
- Shrink and errors hide in weak controls
- Reserves need evidence and policy, not only gut feel
- Systems and warehouse process drive accounting quality

## Verification

- [ ] Valuation method is consistent and documented
- [ ] Reserves have policy and support
- [ ] Count differences are investigated

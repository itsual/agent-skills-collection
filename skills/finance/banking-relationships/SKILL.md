# Banking Relationships

## Overview

Banks provide accounts, payments, credit, and advice. Relationship quality and structure affect pricing, support in stress, and operational resilience.

## When to Use

- Selecting or changing primary banks
- Negotiating credit and fee structures
- Multi-bank strategies for risk management
- Expanding into new geographies

## Core Practices

- Match bank capabilities to company needs (FX, credit, digital, geo)
- Avoid single points of failure for critical payment rails
- Maintain relationship contacts beyond the RM for continuity
- Review fees and services annually
- Prepare materials lenders expect for credit requests

## Principles

- Cheapest is not always best when reliability matters
- Diversification has a cost but reduces operational risk
- Credit is earned in good times for use in bad times
- Clear communication builds goodwill for amendments later

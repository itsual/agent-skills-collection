# Break-even and Contribution

## Overview

Contribution margin and break-even analysis clarify how much volume is needed to cover fixed costs and how profits scale with each additional unit.

## When to Use

- Pricing floor analysis
- New product or channel launch planning
- Cost structure redesign
- Understanding operating leverage

## Core Practices

- Separate variable and fixed costs carefully
- Compute contribution per unit and contribution ratio
- Calculate break-even volume and revenue
- Extend to target-profit volume
- Stress-test fixed cost assumptions and step-costs

## Principles

- Misclassifying costs destroys the analysis
- Step-fixed costs change break-even as you scale
- High operating leverage amplifies both gains and losses
- Multi-product businesses need mix-aware break-even

## Verification

- [ ] Cost classification is explicit and defensible
- [ ] Break-even math is reconcilable
- [ ] Implications for pricing and volume targets are clear

# Startup Runway

## Overview

Runway is how many months the company can operate before cash runs out. It is the fundamental survival metric for pre-profit businesses.

## When to Use

- Monthly cash and burn tracking
- Fundraising timing decisions
- Cost reduction to extend runway
- Board updates on liquidity

## Core Practices

- Define gross burn and net burn clearly
- Calculate runway on conservative cash and burn assumptions
- Scenario plan (base, hire plan, cuts)
- Include committed spends and known cliffs (e.g., annual contracts)
- Start fundraising with adequate runway buffer

## Principles

- Optimistic burn assumptions kill companies
- Runway includes only accessible cash
- Growth plans that ignore runway are fantasies
- Default to raising before desperation shows in terms

## Verification

- [ ] Burn definition is consistent month to month
- [ ] Runway uses realistic forward burn, not only last month
- [ ] Fundraising timeline fits runway buffer

# Cash Flow Management

## Overview

Cash keeps the business alive. Cash flow management covers forecasting receipts and payments, optimizing working capital, and ensuring liquidity under stress.

## When to Use

- Short-term cash forecasting
- Working capital improvement programs
- Liquidity planning for growth or downturns
- Fundraising runway analysis

## Core Practices

- Build direct and/or indirect cash forecasts at the right horizon
- Manage AR (collections), AP (terms), and inventory as a system
- Track cash conversion cycle
- Maintain liquidity buffers and contingency options
- Separate operating cash issues from structural profitability issues

## Principles

- Growth can consume cash even when profitable
- Timing beats totals in short-term survival
- Working capital is often the cheapest “financing”
- Optimistic collection assumptions are a common failure mode

## Verification

- [ ] Cash forecast horizon matches decision needs
- [ ] Working capital drivers are measured
- [ ] Liquidity risks have mitigation plans

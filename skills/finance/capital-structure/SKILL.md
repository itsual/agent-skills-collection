# Capital Structure

## Overview

Capital structure is how a business finances itself. The mix of debt and equity affects risk, control, cost of capital, and flexibility.

## When to Use

- Fundraising and financing decisions
- Debt capacity and refinancing analysis
- Evaluating leverage targets
- Dilution vs debt trade-offs in growth plans

## Core Concepts

- Cost of debt vs cost of equity
- Leverage, coverage, and covenant headroom
- Dilution and ownership impact of equity raises
- Flexibility and refinancing risk
- Optimal structure depends on cash flow stability and growth stage

## Principles

- Cheap capital that creates distress risk is not cheap
- Stage and cash flow predictability constrain leverage
- Covenants and terms matter as much as headline rate
- Align financing with use of proceeds and timeline

## Verification

- [ ] Debt capacity linked to cash flow and covenants
- [ ] Equity dilution scenarios are explicit
- [ ] Financing plan matches strategic horizon

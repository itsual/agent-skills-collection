# Management Reporting

## Overview

Management reporting serves internal decision-makers. It prioritizes speed, relevance, and actionability over exhaustive statutory detail.

## When to Use

- Monthly business reviews
- Weekly flash results
- Department and segment reporting
- Redesigning finance packs for executives

## Core Practices

- Lead with what matters and what changed
- Pair numbers with driver commentary
- Standardize formats so trends are visible
- Align report structure to accountability
- Deliver on a reliable calendar
- Iterate based on what leaders actually use

## Principles

- If nothing would change based on the report, cut it
- Consistency of definitions enables trend reading
- Visual hierarchy beats dense grids for executives
- Timeliness often beats perfection for operational decisions

## Verification

- [ ] Report answers leadership’s real questions
- [ ] Variances and drivers are explained
- [ ] Cadence is reliable

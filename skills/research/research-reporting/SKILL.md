# Research Reporting

## Overview

Research reporting transfers what was learned to people who were not in the room. Great reporting is accurate, scannable, and explicit about confidence.

## When to Use

- Insight readouts and research reports
- Academic and technical write-ups
- Executive summaries of research programs
- Handoffs from research to product/design/policy

## Core Practices

- Lead with answers to the research questions
- Show evidence strength and sample context early
- Use structure: questions → method → findings → implications → limitations
- Visualize where it aids comprehension; don’t decorate
- Separate findings from recommendations
- Archive data and instruments for traceability

## Principles

- Audience determines depth and jargon level
- Surprising findings need stronger evidence display
- Limitations increase trust when stated plainly
- A report that doesn’t change thinking or decisions failed its job

## Verification

- [ ] Research questions are answered explicitly
- [ ] Method and sample are understandable
- [ ] Limitations and next steps are included

# Desk Research

## Overview

Desk research gathers and evaluates existing information. Done well, it answers many questions cheaply and focuses primary research on what remains unknown.

## When to Use

- Rapid market or competitive context
- Fact-checking and background briefs
- Pre-work before interviews or surveys
- Policy, regulatory, or industry scans

## Core Practices

- Clarify the question and “good enough” threshold
- Identify high-yield source types (filings, reports, stats agencies, trade press, academic)
- Triangulate important claims across independent sources
- Date-stamp and quality-rate sources
- Separate facts, estimates, and opinions
- Stop when incremental sources add little new signal

## Principles

- Speed with source hygiene beats endless collection
- Primary-sounding blogs are often secondary opinions
- Numbers without methodology are suspect
- Document paths so others can re-check

## Verification

- [ ] Key claims have sources and dates
- [ ] Conflicts between sources are noted
- [ ] Output answers the original question directly

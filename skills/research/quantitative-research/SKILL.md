# Quantitative Research

## Overview

Quantitative research estimates quantities and tests relationships using structured data. Validity depends on measurement quality, sampling, and honest analysis.

## When to Use

- Sizing markets or segments
- Measuring attitudes, satisfaction, or behavior at scale
- A/B and experiment analysis support
- Testing hypotheses generated qualitatively

## Core Practices

- Operationalize constructs into clear measures
- Plan sampling frame, size, and error tolerance
- Design instruments that minimize bias and ambiguity
- Predefine primary analyses where possible
- Report uncertainty (intervals, not only point estimates)
- Check assumptions and robustness

## Principles

- Precise numbers on biased samples are still biased
- Statistical significance ≠ practical importance
- Multiple comparisons inflate false positives
- Transparency about missing data and exclusions is mandatory

## Verification

- [ ] Measures map cleanly to research questions
- [ ] Sample limitations are stated
- [ ] Results include uncertainty and effect relevance

# Interview Research

## Overview

Research interviews are structured conversations optimized for learning, not selling or confirming. Skillful interviewing balances preparation with responsive listening.

## When to Use

- User and customer discovery interviews
- Expert and stakeholder interviews
- Contextual inquiry follow-ups
- Post-survey deep dives

## Core Practices

- Recruit against criteria tied to the research questions
- Open with consent, purpose, and recording norms
- Ask open questions; prefer “tell me about the last time…”
- Probe for specifics; avoid accepting abstract claims
- Manage time while allowing important tangents
- Debrief and code soon after sessions while memory is fresh

## Principles

- The interviewee should talk most of the time
- Opinions about hypothetical futures are weaker than past behavior
- Neutral stance beats validating the product idea mid-interview
- One brilliant interview is not a sample

## Verification

- [ ] Guide serves the research questions
- [ ] Notes/recordings support evidence-backed themes
- [ ] Recruiting criteria matched the target experiences

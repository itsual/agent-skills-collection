# Ethnographic Research

## Overview

Ethnography studies people in the environments where behavior actually happens. It reveals workarounds, constraints, and social dynamics that self-report misses.

## When to Use

- Complex workflows and workplace studies
- Product use in natural settings
- Service design and multi-actor systems
- Early discovery in unfamiliar domains

## Core Practices

- Define the field site and access ethics clearly
- Observe before intervening; take structured field notes
- Capture context: tools, interruptions, social roles, environment
- Combine observation with contextual inquiry questions
- Triangulate observations across people and moments
- Synthesize patterns of practice, not only anecdotes

## Principles

- What people do often diverges from what they say
- Rapport and respect unlock honest access
- Observation is data; interpretation must be labeled as such
- Small-n depth is the point — not fake generalizability

## Verification

- [ ] Observations are tied to real contexts and times
- [ ] Findings distinguish behavior from stated preference
- [ ] Ethics and consent for observation are handled

# Usability Research

## Overview

Usability research tests whether real people can complete real tasks with an interface. It prioritizes observed behavior over opinion about aesthetics alone.

## When to Use

- Prototype and pre-launch evaluation
- Diagnosing friction in live product flows
- Comparing design alternatives
- Benchmarking task success over time

## Core Practices

- Define critical tasks tied to user goals
- Recruit participants who match the target users
- Use realistic scenarios and data
- Measure success, errors, time, and recovery
- Moderate with minimal leading; capture think-aloud carefully
- Prioritize issues by severity and frequency

## Principles

- Five users often reveal major issues for a given flow — still not statistical proof
- Task success beats “I like it” as evidence
- Severity ratings should reflect user impact and business risk
- Fix-and-retest cycles beat one-shot studies when stakes are high

## Verification

- [ ] Tasks map to real goals
- [ ] Findings are evidenced by observed behavior
- [ ] Recommendations are prioritized and actionable

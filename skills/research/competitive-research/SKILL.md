# Competitive Research

## Overview

Competitive research maps who else solves related problems and how. It supports positioning and product choices when grounded in evidence rather than folklore.

## When to Use

- Market entry or repositioning
- Feature and pricing comparisons
- Win/loss support and sales enablement inputs
- Tracking competitor launches and strategy shifts

## Core Practices

- Define the competitive set including substitutes and status quo
- Collect product, pricing, messaging, and distribution evidence
- Use primary product trials where claims matter
- Separate verified facts from inferred strategy
- Update on a cadence; stale competitive intel misleads
- Translate findings into implications for your moves

## Principles

- Customers’ alternatives matter more than your org chart’s rival list
- Feature matrices without jobs-to-be-done context are shallow
- Public messaging ≠ actual customer experience
- Research should change decisions, not only fill slides

## Verification

- [ ] Competitor set rationale is clear
- [ ] Key claims are sourced and dated
- [ ] Outputs include strategic implications

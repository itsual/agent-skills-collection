# Qualitative Research

## Overview

Qualitative research studies how people experience and make sense of things. It prioritizes depth, context, and meaning over statistical generalizability.

## When to Use

- Problem exploration and discovery
- Understanding “why” behind behaviors
- Generating hypotheses and opportunity areas
- Evaluating concepts with rich feedback

## Core Practices

- Choose methods fit for purpose (interviews, ethnography, diary, focus groups)
- Recruit for relevant diversity of experience, not only convenience
- Use discussion guides that allow emergence, not only rigid scripts
- Record, consent, and store data ethically
- Code and theme systematically; avoid cherry-picking quotes
- Report findings with evidence strength and limitations

## Principles

- Saturation matters more than arbitrary sample size theater
- Researcher bias must be actively managed
- Quotes illustrate themes; they are not the analysis
- Qualitative insight can be rigorous without being quantitative

## Verification

- [ ] Method matches the research questions
- [ ] Analysis process is transparent
- [ ] Findings distinguish observation from interpretation

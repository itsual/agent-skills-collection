# Field Research

## Overview

Field research takes inquiry to the places where work and life happen. It prioritizes ecological validity and often combines observation, interviews, and artifact collection.

## When to Use

- On-site workplace or operational studies
- Home visits and in-context product use
- Ride-alongs and service shadowing
- Environments that remote methods cannot replicate

## Core Practices

- Plan access, safety, and logistics carefully
- Define what must be observed vs asked
- Capture photos/artifacts only with permission
- Minimize disruption to participants’ real work
- Debrief same day while details are fresh
- Synthesize environmental constraints into design implications

## Principles

- Field time is expensive — go with sharp questions
- Safety and respect outrank data completeness
- Context is the data, not background noise
- One site is not a universe — sample sites deliberately when possible

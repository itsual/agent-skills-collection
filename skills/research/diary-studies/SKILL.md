# Diary Studies

## Overview

Diary studies ask participants to record experiences as they happen over days or weeks. They surface patterns across time with less reliance on memory reconstruction.

## When to Use

- Longitudinal product or service experience
- Health, finance, or habit-related behaviors
- Intermittent events (travel, support incidents, purchases)
- Journey research across many touchpoints

## Core Practices

- Define prompts that are specific but not leading
- Choose medium (app, chat, voice, paper) for participant fit
- Set cadence and duration to match the behavior cycle
- Send lightweight reminders without nagging bias
- Plan mid-study check-ins to maintain quality
- Analyze both individual trajectories and cross-participant themes

## Principles

- Participant burden kills completion — keep entries short
- Compliance ≠ honesty; design for ease and motivation
- Incentives should not distort what gets reported
- Time-stamped entries beat end-of-week summaries when possible

## Verification

- [ ] Prompts map to research questions
- [ ] Completion and quality rates are monitored
- [ ] Analysis preserves temporal patterns

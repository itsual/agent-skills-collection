# Product Discovery

## Overview

Discovery is about learning fast and cheaply. The goal is to find evidence that a problem is worth solving and that a solution is desirable, feasible, and viable — before committing major resources.

## When to Use

- Early-stage ideas and opportunities
- Major feature or product bets
- When assumptions about users or value are untested
- Before committing to a full build

## Core Activities

- Problem framing and opportunity assessment
- Customer interviews and observational research
- Assumption mapping and riskiest-assumption tests
- Solution sketching and concept testing
- Prototype and experiment design
- Go / no-go or pivot recommendations

## Principles

- Fall in love with the problem, not the solution
- Test the riskiest assumptions first
- Prefer evidence over opinions
- Time-box discovery; avoid endless research
- Document what you learned and what remains uncertain

## Verification

- [ ] Key assumptions are listed and tested or explicitly accepted
- [ ] Evidence is tied to a clear recommendation
- [ ] Discovery outcomes influence the roadmap or decision

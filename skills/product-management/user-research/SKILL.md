# User Research

## Overview

Good user research answers specific questions that reduce product risk. It is purposeful, rigorous enough for the decision at hand, and translated into implications the team can act on.

## When to Use

- Understanding problems and jobs-to-be-done
- Validating concepts or prototypes
- Exploring usability issues
- Informing prioritization and design decisions

## Core Practices

- Start from a clear research question and decision
- Choose methods that fit the question (interviews, diary studies, usability tests, surveys, etc.)
- Recruit participants who match the target segment
- Separate observation from interpretation
- Synthesize into insights, not just notes
- Tie insights to product implications and next steps

## Principles

- Research is only valuable if it changes a decision or increases confidence
- Avoid leading questions and confirmation bias
- Combine qualitative depth with quantitative signals when possible
- Share findings in a form teams will actually use

## Verification

- [ ] Research question and method are aligned
- [ ] Insights are supported by evidence
- [ ] Implications for the product are explicit

# Stakeholder Management

## Overview

Product work succeeds or fails through people. Stakeholder management is the deliberate practice of mapping influence, aligning incentives, and communicating in ways that build trust and forward motion.

## When to Use

- Complex cross-functional initiatives
- Executive or board-level visibility work
- Conflicting priorities across teams
- Customer or partner commitments with internal implications

## Core Practices

- Map stakeholders by influence and interest
- Clarify decision rights (who decides vs who is consulted)
- Establish communication cadences and artifacts
- Surface disagreements early and structure resolution
- Close the loop when decisions are made
- Protect the team from thrash while staying responsive

## Principles

- Optimize for trust and clarity, not politics for its own sake
- Over-communicate context; under-communicate noise
- Write things down — memory is not a system of record
- Separate advocacy from decision-making roles

## Verification

- [ ] Key stakeholders know the current priorities and rationale
- [ ] Decision owners are clear for major calls
- [ ] Open conflicts are visible and being worked

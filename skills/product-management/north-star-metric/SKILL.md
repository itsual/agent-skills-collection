# North Star Metric

## Overview

A North Star Metric (NSM) captures the value your product delivers to customers in a single, measurable expression. It aligns teams without requiring them to optimize the same tactical KPIs.

## When to Use

- Establishing a primary product success metric
- Aligning multiple teams around shared outcomes
- Reducing metric sprawl and conflicting optimization
- Communicating product health to leadership

## Characteristics of a Good NSM

- Reflects customer value, not just company revenue
- Is measurable with reasonable accuracy
- Can be influenced by product teams
- Leads, rather than only lags, business results
- Is hard to game in destructive ways

## Principles

- Pair the NSM with input metrics and guardrails
- Don’t change the NSM constantly — stability enables focus
- Ensure teams understand how their work moves it
- Review whether the NSM still represents value as the product evolves

## Verification

- [ ] Teams can explain what the NSM means in customer terms
- [ ] Movement in the NSM correlates with real value and business outcomes
- [ ] Guardrail metrics prevent harmful optimization

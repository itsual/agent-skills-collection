# Competitive Analysis

## Overview

Competitive analysis should produce decisions, not just slideware. Focus on how alternatives solve the job, where you win or lose, and what that implies for strategy.

## When to Use

- Strategy and positioning work
- Roadmap and prioritization debates
- Sales enablement and objection handling
- Market entry or pivot decisions

## Core Practices

- Define the real alternatives (including status quo and indirect competitors)
- Compare on jobs-to-be-done, not only feature checklists
- Assess strengths, weaknesses, pricing, and go-to-market motion
- Identify differentiation that matters to customers
- Translate findings into product, messaging, or sales actions

## Principles

- Customers buy progress, not features — analyze through that lens
- Update analysis as the market moves; stale comps mislead
- Avoid obsession with competitors at the expense of customers
- Be honest about where you are behind

## Verification

- [ ] Alternatives considered include non-obvious ones
- [ ] Implications for product or positioning are explicit
- [ ] Evidence is current enough to trust

# Metrics and KPIs

## Overview

Good metrics connect product work to customer and business outcomes. They should be actionable, hard to game, and understood by the people who use them.

## When to Use

- Defining success for a product, feature, or experiment
- Building product dashboards and reviews
- Diagnosing performance issues
- Aligning stakeholders on goals

## Core Practices

- Distinguish input metrics, output metrics, and guardrail metrics
- Prefer rates and cohorts over raw counts when appropriate
- Define metric formulas, data sources, and owners explicitly
- Pair North Star / primary metrics with counter-metrics
- Review metrics on a cadence and retire ones that no longer drive decisions

## Principles

- Measure outcomes, not just activity
- A metric is only useful if it can change a decision
- Watch for Goodhart’s law — when a measure becomes a target, it can cease to be a good measure
- Make confidence in the data visible

## Verification

- [ ] Primary metrics map to product strategy
- [ ] Definitions are clear and reproducible
- [ ] Teams know how to influence the metrics they are accountable for

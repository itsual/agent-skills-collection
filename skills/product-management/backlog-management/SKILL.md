# Backlog Management

## Overview

The backlog is the operational expression of product priority. A healthy backlog is ordered, understandable, and continuously refined — not an infinite wish list.

## When to Use

- Ongoing product ownership and intake
- Sprint / iteration preparation
- Roadmap-to-execution translation
- Reducing thrash and surprise work

## Core Practices

- Clear intake and prioritization process
- Regular refinement with design and engineering
- Stories sized and clarified just in time
- Explicit ordering (not everything is #1)
- Separation of discovery work, delivery work, and debt
- Periodic pruning of stale items

## Principles

- If everything is high priority, nothing is
- Backlog items should be ready enough for the next planning horizon — not fully specified years ahead
- Make dependencies and risks visible
- Protect capacity for unplanned but important work

## Verification

- [ ] Top of backlog is well-understood by the delivery team
- [ ] Ordering reflects current strategy and evidence
- [ ] Stale or irrelevant items are removed or deferred explicitly

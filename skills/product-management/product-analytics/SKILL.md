# Product Analytics

## Overview

Product analytics turns usage into insight. Done well, it answers what users do, where they struggle, and whether changes improved outcomes.

## When to Use

- Defining instrumentation for new features
- Analyzing activation, retention, and engagement
- Measuring impact after launches
- Exploring user behavior to generate hypotheses

## Core Practices

- Define a clear analytics tracking plan (events, properties, ownership)
- Focus on a small set of meaningful funnels and retention views
- Segment by cohort, persona proxy, or acquisition source when relevant
- Combine quantitative patterns with qualitative research
- Establish baselines before judging experiments or launches

## Principles

- Instrument for decisions, not for collecting everything possible
- Protect data quality — bad events create false confidence
- Look at trends and cohorts, not only snapshots
- Privacy and compliance constraints are part of the design

## Verification

- [ ] Key product questions can be answered with available data
- [ ] Event definitions are documented and trusted
- [ ] Insights regularly appear in product decisions

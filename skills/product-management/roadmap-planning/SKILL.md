# Roadmap Planning

## Overview

A roadmap is a strategic communication tool, not a project plan. It shows where you are going and why, while preserving flexibility in the how and when.

## When to Use

- Setting or revising product direction for a quarter or longer
- Aligning leadership, engineering, design, and go-to-market
- Communicating priorities to customers or partners (when appropriate)

## Core Practices

- Anchor the roadmap in strategy and outcomes, not a feature list
- Use themes or outcomes as the primary organizing layer
- Indicate confidence and horizon (now / next / later)
- Keep date commitments rare and intentional
- Update the roadmap as learning changes priorities
- Make trade-offs and dependencies visible

## Principles

- Roadmaps should enable conversation, not shut it down
- Prefer outcome-based roadmaps over output-based ones
- Protect capacity for discovery and unplanned work
- Version and communicate changes clearly

## Verification

- [ ] A reader can see the strategic logic of the sequence
- [ ] Near-term items are more concrete than later ones
- [ ] Stakeholders understand what is committed vs directional

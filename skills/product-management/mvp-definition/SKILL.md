# MVP Definition

## Overview

MVP is about learning, not shipping a worse product forever. The goal is the smallest thing that delivers real value (or valid learning) to real users.

## When to Use

- Scoping a new product or major capability
- Reducing scope to hit a meaningful deadline
- Designing a pilot or beta
- Aligning teams on what is essential vs later

## Core Practices

- State the primary hypothesis or outcome the MVP must test/deliver
- Identify the thinnest end-to-end path that creates value
- Explicitly list what is out of scope and why
- Define success metrics and feedback channels
- Plan the iteration path after the MVP

## Principles

- Viable means valuable to someone — not just buildable
- Cut scope by narrowing users, jobs, or platforms before degrading quality of the core experience
- Avoid “MVP” as an excuse for sloppy work on the critical path
- Agree on learning goals before build starts

## Verification

- [ ] Core hypothesis or outcome is clear
- [ ] Out-of-scope items are explicit
- [ ] Success criteria for the MVP are defined

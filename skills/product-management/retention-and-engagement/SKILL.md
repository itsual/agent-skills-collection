# Retention and Engagement

## Overview

Acquisition is wasted without retention. Strong products create repeated value and habits; weak ones become a revolving door.

## When to Use

- Analyzing retention cohorts and churn
- Improving habit formation or core loop frequency
- Designing engagement features or lifecycle messaging
- Diagnosing why users drop off after signup or first use

## Core Practices

- Define retention for your product (e.g., D1/D7/D30, weekly active)
- Segment cohorts by acquisition source, persona, or behavior
- Identify the activation behaviors that predict retention
- Map the core usage loop and friction within it
- Design interventions (product, messaging, education) for specific drop-off points

## Principles

- Retention problems are often activation or value problems in disguise
- Frequency should match the natural cadence of the job-to-be-done
- Don’t confuse notification volume with engagement quality
- Measure habit and depth, not only logins

## Verification

- [ ] Retention definition is clear and agreed
- [ ] Leading indicators of retention are identified
- [ ] Interventions target specific, evidenced drop-offs

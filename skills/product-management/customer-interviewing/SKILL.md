# Customer Interviewing

## Overview

Great interviews reveal what people actually do and why. Poor interviews collect compliments and hypotheticals that mislead product decisions.

## When to Use

- Problem and opportunity discovery
- Validating demand or willingness to pay signals
- Understanding workflows and workarounds
- Testing concepts without over-leading the participant

## Core Practices

- Define the interview goal and decision it informs
- Recruit participants who match the target segment
- Use open, behavior-focused questions (“Tell me about the last time…”)
- Avoid pitching your solution early
- Listen for workarounds, emotions, and switching triggers
- Take structured notes; synthesize across interviews

## Principles

- Past behavior > future hypotheticals
- Silence is a tool — don’t fill every gap
- One interview is an anecdote; patterns across interviews are insight
- Separate what you heard from what you concluded

## Verification

- [ ] Questions map to a clear research goal
- [ ] Notes distinguish quotes/observations from interpretations
- [ ] Synthesis produces implications for the product

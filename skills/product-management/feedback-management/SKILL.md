# Feedback Management

## Overview

Feedback is abundant; insight is rare. Effective feedback management captures input, finds patterns, and routes it into discovery and prioritization — while closing the loop with those who contributed.

## When to Use

- Setting up or improving feedback channels
- Triaging feature requests and complaints
- Connecting support, sales, and success insights to product
- Demonstrating responsiveness to customers

## Core Practices

- Multiple input channels with clear ownership
- Tagging and categorization for pattern detection
- Regular synthesis into themes and opportunities
- Explicit link from themes to roadmap or discovery decisions
- Closing the loop when feedback influences outcomes
- Avoiding the “loudest customer wins” trap

## Principles

- One request is an anecdote; patterns are evidence
- Separate severity (bugs) from popularity (requests)
- Don’t promise timelines in response to every request
- Thank people and follow up when you ship related improvements

## Verification

- [ ] Feedback is visible and searchable by the product team
- [ ] Themes influence prioritization discussions
- [ ] Customers sometimes hear back when their input mattered

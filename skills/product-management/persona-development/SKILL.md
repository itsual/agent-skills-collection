# Persona Development

## Overview

Personas are decision tools, not art projects. Good personas capture goals, behaviors, constraints, and contexts that change what you build and how you prioritize.

## When to Use

- Synthesizing user research into shareable artifacts
- Aligning product, design, and go-to-market on target users
- Guiding prioritization and UX decisions
- Onboarding new team members to the customer base

## Core Elements

- Segment and distinguishing characteristics
- Goals and jobs-to-be-done
- Behaviors and workflows
- Pain points and constraints
- Environment and context of use
- Success criteria from the user’s perspective
- (Optional) Quote or evidence anchors from research

## Principles

- Base personas on evidence, not stereotypes
- Prefer fewer, sharper personas over many vague ones
- Focus on differences that change product decisions
- Keep personas living — update when research warrants it
- Avoid fictional biographies that don’t affect decisions

## Verification

- [ ] Personas are traceable to research or data
- [ ] They help resolve real product trade-offs
- [ ] Teams actually reference them in discussions

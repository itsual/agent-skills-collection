# Onboarding Optimization

## Overview

Onboarding is the critical path from signup to experienced value. Great onboarding is personalized, progressive, and focused on the activation actions that predict long-term retention.

## When to Use

- Low activation or poor new-user retention
- Launching onboarding for a new product or segment
- Reducing time-to-value
- Improving trial-to-paid conversion

## Core Practices

- Define the activation event(s) that predict retention
- Map the first-session and first-week journey
- Remove steps that don’t contribute to activation
- Use progressive disclosure and just-in-time guidance
- Personalize paths by role, use case, or intent when data allows
- Instrument every step of the onboarding funnel

## Principles

- Optimize for time-to-value, not completeness of education
- Empty states and setup friction are first-class product problems
- One successful outcome beats a tour of every feature
- Continuously test; onboarding decays as the product changes

## Verification

- [ ] Activation metric is defined and measured
- [ ] Major drop-off points are identified and owned
- [ ] Onboarding changes are evaluated against activation and retention

# Workforce Planning

## Overview

Workforce planning connects business strategy to people capacity. It anticipates what roles and skills will be needed and how to obtain them.

## When to Use

- Annual and multi-year headcount planning
- Skill-gap analysis for strategic capabilities
- Location and remote/hybrid workforce design
- Build vs buy vs automate decisions

## Core Practices

- Translate business plans into role and skill demand
- Assess current supply, attrition risk, and productivity assumptions
- Identify gaps and scenarios (growth, flat, contraction)
- Plan actions: hire, upskill, redeploy, contractor, outsource
- Align with finance on budget and timing
- Update plans as business conditions change

## Principles

- Headcount is not the only capacity lever
- Lag in hiring means plan ahead of demand spikes
- Critical skills need longer lead times
- Planning quality depends on honest attrition and productivity data

## Verification

- [ ] Demand assumptions link to business plans
- [ ] Gap actions are prioritized and owned
- [ ] Finance and HR plans are reconciled

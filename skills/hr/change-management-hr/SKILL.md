# Change Management (HR)

## Overview

Most changes fail on the people side, not the plan side. HR change management builds awareness, desire, knowledge, ability, and reinforcement so new ways of working stick.

## When to Use

- Restructures and operating model changes
- HRIS and process transformations
- Cultural or leadership shifts
- M&A people integration

## Core Practices

- Stakeholder and impact analysis early
- Communication that answers “why,” “what,” and “what it means for me”
- Manager enablement as the primary cascade channel
- Training and support at points of need
- Feedback loops during rollout
- Reinforcement until new behaviors stabilize

## Principles

- Announcement ≠ adoption
- Resistance often signals unaddressed risk or loss
- Middle managers make or break change
- Change capacity is finite — sequence initiatives

## Verification

- [ ] Impacted groups and impacts are mapped
- [ ] Managers are equipped to lead the change
- [ ] Adoption metrics are defined and tracked

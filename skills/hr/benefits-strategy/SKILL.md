# Benefits Strategy

## Overview

Benefits are part of total rewards and employee value proposition. Strategy prioritizes what the workforce values and what the organization can sustain.

## When to Use

- Benefits portfolio redesign
- Vendor selection and renewals
- Wellbeing program strategy
- Supporting diverse workforce needs (families, locations, life stages)

## Core Practices

- Understand utilization and perceived value, not only cost
- Benchmark thoughtfully against relevant talent markets
- Design for inclusion across life stages and family structures
- Communicate benefits clearly — unused benefits waste spend
- Measure wellbeing and retention signals tied to programs
- Manage compliance and fiduciary duties where applicable

## Principles

- More benefits ≠ better benefits
- Communication is part of program design
- One-size rarely fits multi-country or multi-generational workforces
- Cost trend management is continuous, not annual panic

## Verification

- [ ] Priority benefits link to talent strategy
- [ ] Utilization and feedback inform changes
- [ ] Compliance obligations are owned

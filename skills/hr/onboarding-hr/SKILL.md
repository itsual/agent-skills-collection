# Onboarding (HR)

## Overview

HR onboarding covers the full joining experience: preboarding, first-day readiness, role ramp, culture integration, and early checkpoints. It is a retention control as much as an admin process.

## When to Use

- Redesigning new-hire programs
- Reducing early attrition
- Standardizing manager onboarding responsibilities
- Scaling hiring volume without chaos

## Core Practices

- Start before day one (preboarding logistics and welcome)
- Ensure tools, access, and intro meetings are ready on day one
- Define 30/60/90 expectations with the manager
- Assign buddies/mentors where helpful
- Check in on belonging and clarity, not only tasks
- Collect new-hire feedback and fix systemic gaps

## Principles

- First-week friction predicts disengagement
- Manager ownership beats HR-only orientation
- Onboarding is role-specific, not only company-generic
- Early feedback is cheaper than exit interviews

## Verification

- [ ] Day-one readiness checklist is reliable
- [ ] 30/60/90 plans are expected for roles
- [ ] Early attrition reasons are analyzed

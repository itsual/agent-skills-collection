# HR Policies

## Overview

HR policies set expectations for conduct, time off, workplace rules, and people processes. Clarity and consistency reduce disputes and legal risk.

## When to Use

- Handbook creation or overhaul
- New policy introduction (remote work, AI use, etc.)
- Policy conflict resolution
- Manager enablement on applying policies

## Core Practices

- Write in plain language for the intended audience
- Align policy with actual practice or change practice deliberately
- Define scope, eligibility, process, and exceptions
- Version and communicate changes with acknowledgment when needed
- Train managers on high-judgment policies
- Review periodically for legal and operational fit

## Principles

- Unenforceable policies create selective enforcement risk
- Over-detailed policies can reduce needed judgment — balance
- Accessibility of the current version matters
- Legal review is essential for high-risk topics

## Verification

- [ ] Policies are findable and current
- [ ] Managers know how to apply key policies
- [ ] Exceptions are governed

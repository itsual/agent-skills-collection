# Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion

## Overview

DEI work aims for fair access to opportunity and a workplace where people can contribute fully. Durable progress comes from process design and accountability, not only events.

## When to Use

- DEI strategy and goal setting
- Hiring and promotion process equity reviews
- Pay equity analysis support
- Inclusive culture and belonging initiatives

## Core Practices

- Define what outcomes matter (representation, pay equity, belonging, advancement)
- Diagnose process points where bias and barriers appear
- Redesign processes (structured hiring, transparent criteria, sponsorship)
- Set goals with data and ownership
- Measure progress and publish accountability appropriately
- Avoid performative activity that doesn’t change outcomes

## Principles

- Representation without inclusion does not retain talent
- Equity is about fair process and outcomes, not identical treatment in all cases
- Legal compliance and DEI goals must be designed carefully by jurisdiction
- Leadership accountability determines whether programs stick

## Verification

- [ ] Outcome metrics are defined
- [ ] Process interventions target diagnosed barriers
- [ ] Progress is reviewed on a cadence

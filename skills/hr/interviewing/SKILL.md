# Interviewing

## Overview

Structured interviewing improves prediction and fairness versus unstructured chat. Good interviews collect comparable evidence against defined competencies.

## When to Use

- Designing interview loops for a role
- Training hiring managers and interviewers
- Reducing bias and noise in selection
- Debrief and decision quality improvement

## Core Practices

- Define competencies tied to role success
- Use consistent question banks and scoring rubrics
- Probe for specific past behavior and outcomes
- Take evidence notes, not only impressions
- Debrief with structured comparison across candidates
- Separate “culture add” from affinity bias

## Principles

- Gut feel is data — weak data
- Same questions enable fair comparison
- Interviewer training is a control, not optional polish
- One strong interviewer cannot compensate for a chaotic loop

## Verification

- [ ] Competencies and rubrics exist for the role
- [ ] Interviewers are briefed and calibrated
- [ ] Decisions reference scored evidence

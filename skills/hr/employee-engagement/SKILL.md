# Employee Engagement

## Overview

Engagement reflects whether people are willing to give discretionary energy. Measurement without action breeds cynicism; action without measurement is guesswork.

## When to Use

- Engagement or pulse survey programs
- Action planning after results
- Manager accountability for team climate
- Linking engagement to retention outcomes

## Core Practices

- Measure with validated, actionable items
- Segment results to find real variation
- Prioritize a few drivers, not 40 initiatives
- Push action ownership to managers with support
- Close the loop with employees on what changed
- Track engagement alongside turnover and productivity signals

## Principles

- Survey fatigue follows ignored results
- Company-wide averages hide team-level truth
- Pay and role clarity often dominate “free fruit” perks
- Senior behavior sets the ceiling on engagement

## Verification

- [ ] Survey cadence and response process are reliable
- [ ] Action plans exist for priority areas
- [ ] Follow-through is visible to employees

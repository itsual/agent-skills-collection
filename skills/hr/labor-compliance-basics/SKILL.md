# Labor Compliance Basics

## Overview

Employment law obligations vary by jurisdiction and are high-stakes. HR generalists need enough literacy to design compliant processes and escalate correctly — not to replace employment counsel.

## When to Use

- Designing timekeeping, leave, and pay processes
- Worker classification questions (employee vs contractor)
- Multi-state or multi-country employment basics
- Policy design with legal implications

## Core Practices

- Know which topics always need legal review in your context
- Maintain jurisdiction-aware policy variants where required
- Document working time and leave reliably
- Treat classification decisions as risk decisions with criteria
- Train managers on high-risk actions (termination, accommodation)
- Monitor regulatory changes affecting employment rules

## Principles

- Templates from one country do not travel automatically
- “Everyone does it” is not a legal defense
- Early legal involvement is cheaper than remediation
- Recordkeeping is often the difference in disputes

## Verification

- [ ] High-risk processes have legal review checkpoints
- [ ] Recordkeeping supports wage/hour and leave compliance
- [ ] Escalation path to counsel is clear

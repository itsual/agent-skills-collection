# Employer Branding

## Overview

Employer branding shapes how potential and current employees perceive the organization as a place to work. Authenticity outperforms polish that reality cannot support.

## When to Use

- Careers site and recruiting campaigns
- Differentiating in competitive talent markets
- Aligning brand promises with employee experience
- Reputation recovery after public workplace issues

## Core Practices

- Ground messages in real employee experience data
- Segment narratives for priority talent audiences
- Use employee voices and concrete examples
- Align external brand with internal reality
- Measure funnel quality and offer acceptance, not only impressions
- Coordinate HR, marketing, and leadership messaging

## Principles

- Overpromising increases early attrition
- Glassdoor and social proof are part of the brand whether managed or not
- Different roles may need different brand emphasis
- Current employees are the most credible messengers

## Verification

- [ ] Brand claims are evidence-backed
- [ ] Priority audiences are defined
- [ ] Recruiting outcomes are linked to brand efforts

# Manager Enablement

## Overview

Managers are the primary people experience for most employees. Enablement treats management as a craft to be trained, supported, and held accountable — not an accidental title.

## When to Use

- New manager programs
- Improving inconsistent people leadership quality
- Rolling out new people processes through managers
- Reducing manager-driven attrition

## Core Practices

- Define what great management means in observable behaviors
- Train at moments of need (first team, first hire, first termination)
- Provide tools: conversation guides, checklists, decision supports
- Create peer learning and coaching channels for managers
- Include people-leadership in manager performance evaluation
- Remove administrative burden that crowds out leading

## Principles

- Promoting top ICs without enablement creates accidental managers
- One training event does not create capability
- Accountability without support is unfair; support without accountability is ineffective
- Manager quality is a leading indicator of engagement and retention

## Verification

- [ ] New managers receive structured enablement
- [ ] Tools and guidance are accessible in the flow of work
- [ ] Manager effectiveness is measured somehow (not only revenue)

# Learning and Development

## Overview

L&D closes skill gaps that strategy depends on. Effective L&D prioritizes critical capabilities, uses appropriate modalities, and measures application — not only attendance.

## When to Use

- Learning strategy and priority skills
- Leadership development programs
- Role-based academies and upskilling
- Evaluating L&D vendors and content

## Core Practices

- Start from capability needs linked to strategy
- Blend learning: experience, exposure, education (70/20/10 mindset)
- Design for application and manager support post-training
- Prioritize ruthlessly — not every request is strategic
- Measure behavior change and business impact where feasible
- Maintain accessible learning pathways for career growth

## Principles

- Training without practice decays fast
- Manager reinforcement multiplies L&D ROI
- Completions are a weak success metric alone
- Internal experts are often underused teachers

## Verification

- [ ] Priority capabilities are defined
- [ ] Programs have application plans, not only events
- [ ] Feedback and impact signals inform iteration

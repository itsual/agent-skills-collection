# Workplace Investigations

## Overview

Workplace investigations establish facts so the organization can respond appropriately. Process quality protects complainants, respondents, and the organization.

## When to Use

- Harassment, discrimination, or serious misconduct allegations
- Policy breach fact-finding
- Retaliation concerns
- Determining credibility and outcomes

## Core Practices

- Scope the allegation and choose investigator without conflicts
- Plan interviews and evidence collection
- Maintain appropriate confidentiality
- Assess credibility with reasoned analysis
- Document findings separately from disciplinary decisions when appropriate
- Protect against retaliation throughout and after

## Principles

- Presumption of fair process for all parties
- Thoroughness should match severity and complexity
- Delay can harm people and evidence quality
- Legal counsel involvement is often warranted on sensitive matters

## Verification

- [ ] Investigator independence is considered
- [ ] Findings are evidence-based and documented
- [ ] Retaliation monitoring is in place

# Wellbeing and Burnout

## Overview

Burnout is often a system outcome: chronic overload, low control, unfairness, or values conflict. Sustainable wellbeing work changes work design, not only offers meditation apps.

## When to Use

- Rising burnout or exhaustion signals
- Wellbeing strategy design
- Post-crisis or high-growth stress periods
- Manager guidance on sustainable performance

## Core Practices

- Monitor leading indicators (hours, after-hours work, attrition, engagement items)
- Address workload and staffing root causes
- Train managers to spot and discuss strain early
- Provide accessible mental health and support resources
- Protect recovery time and reasonable boundaries
- Avoid wellbeing-washing that ignores structural issues

## Principles

- Individual resilience cannot fix chronic understaffing
- Stigma blocks help-seeking — leadership openness matters
- Performance cultures can still be humane
- Prevention is cheaper than replacement and recovery

## Verification

- [ ] Burnout risk signals are monitored
- [ ] Work design actions exist alongside resources
- [ ] Managers know how to respond to strain

# Org Design Basics

## Overview

Organizational design shapes how work gets coordinated. Structure should follow strategy and work processes — not only politics or historical accident.

## When to Use

- Restructures and team redesigns
- Span of control and layer reviews
- Clarifying accountabilities (RACI-style)
- Post-growth or post-merger organization cleanup

## Core Practices

- Start from strategy and core workflows
- Define decision rights and interfaces between teams
- Right-size spans and layers for the work type
- Minimize duplicate ownership and gaps
- Sequence structural change with people transitions
- Reassess after implementation — design is a hypothesis

## Principles

- Structure cannot fix broken strategy or process alone
- Every matrix has a coordination cost
- Clarity of ownership beats elegant charts
- Change structure sparingly; churn has a cost

## Verification

- [ ] Design choices link to strategy/work needs
- [ ] Accountabilities are explicit at interfaces
- [ ] Implementation includes people transition plans
